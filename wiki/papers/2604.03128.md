# Self-Distilled RLVR

**Published**: 2026-04-02 | **Added**: 2026-04-08 | **arXiv**: [2604.03128](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.03128)

## Summary

This paper addresses a critical failure mode in On-Policy Self-Distillation (OPSD), where a single model serves as both teacher and student with the teacher receiving privileged information (such as reference answers). The authors prove that OPSD suffers from an irreducible mutual information gap I(Yt;R|X, Y<t)>0 caused by information asymmetry between teacher and student, which drives privileged information leakage and progressive performance degradation.

The proposed RLSD (RLVR with Self-Distillation) fundamentally rethinks the role of self-distillation: instead of using the teacher to generate token-level distributions for distribution matching (which causes leakage), RLSD repurposes self-distillation for token-level credit assignment while anchoring update directions to environment rewards. The method achieves 56.18% average accuracy across five multimodal reasoning benchmarks, outperforming the base LLM by 4.69% and GRPO by 2.32%, with sustained improvement without the early-peak-then-degrade pattern observed in OPSD.

The core insight is that direction and magnitude signals have asymmetric requirements: the directional signal can be sparse but must be reliable (an erroneous direction harms the policy), while the magnitude signal benefits from being as dense as possible for fine-grained discrimination among tokens. RLSD uses environment rewards for direction and the privileged teacher's evidence ratio for magnitude modulation.

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical Analysis**: Proved that distribution matching under information asymmetry is ill-posed, containing an irreducible mutual information gap that drives privileged information leakage through the gradient structure
- **Gradient Decomposition**: Showed that per-sample gradients carry an R-specific deviation whose variance is proportional to the mutual information gap, causing early gains followed by systematic degradation
- **RLSD Framework**: Repurposes self-distillation from generative target to magnitude evaluator; environment reward determines update direction, privileged teacher modulates update magnitude
- **Leakage-Free Guarantee**: RLSD is structurally immune to privileged information leakage because the privileged information only enters as a stop-gradient scalar multiplier, never influencing gradient direction
- **Strong Empirical Results**: 56.18% average accuracy, +4.69% over base LLM, +2.32% over GRPO, with faster convergence

## Method

### Why OPSD Fails: The Ill-Posed Objective

In OPSD, the teacher conditions on privileged information R (e.g., a verified reasoning trace) that the student cannot observe. This creates fundamental information asymmetry:

**Theorem 1 (KL Decomposition)**: The OPSD objective decomposes as:
```
LOPSD = L* + I(Yt;R|X, Y<t)
```
where L* is the ideal marginal matching objective and I(Yt;R|X, Y<t) is the conditional mutual information between the current token and privileged information. Critically, this gap is independent of θ—the student's optimization cannot eliminate it.

**Per-Sample Gradient Decomposition**: The gradient splits into:
- g*(θ): marginal matching (beneficial component)
- δ(θ;r): r-specific deviation (pathological component)

Early in training, ||g*(θ)|| >> ||δ(θ;r)||, so the beneficial component dominates. As training progresses and PS approaches the marginal teacher distribution, ||g*(θ)|| → 0 while ||δ(θ;r)|| remains bounded away from zero (its variance is governed by the θ-independent mutual information). Parameter updates become dominated by δ, driving the model toward encoding X→R correlations.

### RLSD Solution

RLSD decouples the roles of environment reward and privileged teacher:

**Step 1 - Privileged Information Gain**:
```
Δt = sg(log PT(yt) - log PS(yt))
```
This isolates the marginal contribution of privileged information R to each token prediction.

**Step 2 - Direction-Aware Evidence Reweighting**:
```
wt = exp(sign(A) · Δt) = (PT(yt)/PS(yt))^sign(A)
```
When A > 0 (correct trajectory), wt = PT/PS: tokens supported by R receive larger weights. When A < 0 (incorrect), wt = PS/PT: tokens disfavored by R bear greater blame.

**Step 3 - Clipped Credit Assignment**:
```
Ât = A · clip(wt, 1-εw, 1+εw)
```
Bounding the maximum influence of any single token, analogous to PPO's importance ratio clipping.

**Key Property**: sign(Ât) = sign(A) for all t—the environment reward has exclusive authority over update direction. The teacher only modulates relative intensity within a trajectory.

### Integration with GRPO

RLSD is a drop-in replacement for the uniform advantage in standard GRPO. The only modification is internal credit redistribution within each trajectory. The additional computational cost is one forward pass per response to obtain teacher logits.

## Results

### Main Results on Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct

| Method | MMMU | MathVista | MathVision | ZeroBench | WeMath | Average |
|--------|------|-----------|------------|-----------|--------|---------|
| Base LLM | 62.44 | 73.80 | 47.37 | 19.76 | 54.10 | 51.49 |
| GRPO | 65.11 | 76.20 | 48.82 | 22.60 | 56.57 | 53.86 |
| OPSD | 63.82 | 75.10 | 47.53 | 21.06 | 54.95 | 52.49 |
| SDPO | 65.11 | 74.00 | 47.27 | 25.15 | 52.19 | 52.74 |
| GRPO+OPSD | 63.22 | 75.90 | 48.52 | 22.16 | 54.76 | 52.91 |
| **RLSD** | **67.22** | **78.10** | **52.73** | **24.85** | **58.00** | **56.18** |

RLSD outperforms GRPO by 2.32% on average, with particularly strong gains on MathVision (+3.91%) and WeMath (+1.43%) where fine-grained discrimination of reasoning steps matters most.

### Training Dynamics

- **OPSD**: Peaks at 10-20 steps, then degrades monotonically due to accumulating leakage
- **RLSD**: Shows steeper initial ascent and converges to higher ceiling than GRPO without OPSD's collapse
- **KL Divergence**: OPSD stagnates at initial level; OPD (with external teacher) decreases steadily
- **Entropy**: GRPO suffers rapid entropy collapse; RLSD maintains higher entropy by selectively strengthening critical tokens

### Case Study: Token-Level Credit Heatmaps

In correct trajectories, RLSD assigns larger credit to decisive steps (e.g., identifying relevant objects, executing calculations). In incorrect trajectories, the strongest blame concentrates on misread relations and derived wrong answers. RLSD redistributes sequence-level reward to token level based on each token's Bayesian evidence for the correct answer.

## Limitations & Future Work

The current paper focuses primarily on multimodal reasoning scenarios. Preliminary experiments on pure text reasoning, video understanding, and additional model families beyond Qwen show consistent gains. The theoretical analysis applies to any distribution matching under information asymmetry, suggesting broad applicability.

The authors note that RLSD requires only the final ground-truth answer as privileged information (unlike OPSD which needs reasoning traces), making it less demanding in terms of data preparation.
