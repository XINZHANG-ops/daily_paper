# Claw-Eval: Toward Trustworthy Evaluation of Autonomous Agents

**Published**: 2026-04-06 | **Added**: 2026-04-08 | **arXiv**: [2604.06132](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.06132)

## Summary

Claw-Eval addresses three critical failures in existing agent evaluation: trajectory-opaque grading that misses 44% of safety violations, underspecified safety/robustness evaluation, and narrow modality coverage. The framework introduces full-trajectory auditing via three independent evidence channels (execution traces, audit logs, environment snapshots), integrated multi-dimensional scoring (Completion, Safety, Robustness), and unified cross-modal coverage across 300 tasks. Key findings include that question quality (r=0.87) predicts multi-turn success far better than quantity (r=0.07), and no single model dominates all modalities—each domain (Video, Doc & Image, Code) has a different leader.

## Key Contributions

- Three independent evidence channels for full-trajectory auditing: execution traces, audit logs, environment snapshots
- Integrated multi-dimensional scoring: Completion, Safety, and Robustness (vs single-metric accuracy)
- Unified cross-modal coverage across 300 tasks spanning 9 categories and 3 groups
- Reveals that trajectory-opaque evaluation misses 44% of safety violations and 13% of robustness failures

## Method

Claw-Eval executes agents through a three-phase lifecycle (Setup, Execution, Judge) in isolated Docker containers. During execution, all three evidence channels record independently. During judging, a hybrid pipeline combines output checking (via rubric), execution auditing (via trace/log analysis), and environment verification (via snapshot comparison). Multi-trial evaluation (k=3) reports Average Score, Pass@3, and Pass^3. The Pass^3 metric (all three trials must succeed) proves far more discriminative than Pass@3 (any one trial succeeds).

## Results

Across 14 frontier models, trajectory-opaque evaluation misses 44% of safety violations and 13% of robustness failures. Error injection degrades Pass^3 by up to 24% while Pass@3 remains stable—showing Pass@3 is insufficiently discriminative. Question quality (r=0.87) predicts multi-turn success far better than quantity (r=0.07). No single model dominates all modalities; Gemini-3-Pro leads Video, different models lead Doc & Image and Code.

## Limitations & Future Work

The 300-task scope may not generalize to all agent domains. Docker-based evaluation limits testing to software environments; physical world agents require different infrastructure.
