# TriAttention: Efficient Long Reasoning with Trigonometric KV Compression

**Published**: 2026-04-05 | **Added**: 2026-04-07 | **arXiv**: [2604.04921](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.04921)

## Summary

TriAttention addresses the severe KV cache memory bottleneck in long chain-of-thought reasoning for large language models. The key insight is that existing KV cache compression methods estimate token importance from post-RoPE attention scores, but queries rotate with position during RoPE, creating a tiny observation window that causes poor top-key selection and unstable reasoning. The paper discovers "Q/K concentration" in pre-RoPE space: Q and K vectors are highly concentrated around fixed non-zero centers and remain stable across positions. This concentration causes attention to follow predictable distance preferences via a trigonometric series, enabling reliable key importance estimation from stable Q/K centers rather than volatile post-RoPE observations.

TriAttention scores keys using the trigonometric series derived from Q/K centers plus norm-based signals, with adaptive weighting by concentration. On AIME25 with 32K-token generation, TriAttention matches Full Attention accuracy (40.8%) while achieving 2.5× higher throughput or 10.7× KV memory reduction, while baselines like R-KV achieve only ~20% accuracy at the same efficiency.

## Key Contributions

- **Q/K Concentration Discovery**: Pre-RoPE Q/K vectors are highly concentrated around fixed non-zero centers, stable across positions and contexts (measured by Mean Resultant Length R approaching 1.0 for most heads)
- **Trigonometric Series Connection**: When Q/K are concentrated, attention computation reduces to a trigonometric series in Q-K distance, enabling predictable distance preferences from Q/K centers alone
- **Superior Efficiency-Accuracy Trade-off**: At equivalent accuracy, achieves 2.5× throughput or 10.7× KV memory reduction; R-KV achieves only half the accuracy at same efficiency
- **Cross-Architecture Validation**: Q/K concentration verified across Qwen3, Qwen2.5, Llama3 (GQA) and GLM-4.7 (MLA) architectures
- **Real-World Deployment**: Enables OpenClaw deployment on single RTX 4090 (24GB) with Qwen3-32B (INT4), where Full Attention causes OOM

## Method

**Problem with Post-RoPE Methods**:
- Post-RoPE queries rotate with position as RoPE applies position-dependent rotations
- Only the most recent queries retain up-to-date orientations, forming a tiny observation window
- With few representative queries, important keys go undetected—a token receiving low attention during the short window may be permanently evicted
- Retrieval heads particularly suffer: relevant tokens can remain dormant for long periods before becoming essential
- Prior work confirms performance peaks around 25 queries (a tiny fraction of typical long contexts) and declines thereafter

**Q/K Concentration Phenomenon**:
- Pre-RoPE Q/K vectors cluster around fixed non-zero centers across most attention heads
- Measured by Mean Resultant Length R = ∥E[q]∥/E[∥q∥], where R→1 indicates perfect concentration
- On Qwen3-8B, vast majority of heads exhibit R values approaching 1.0
- Concentration is model-intrinsic: measured across Math, Coding, and Chat domains yields nearly identical values (0.977–0.980), with ~90% of heads exhibiting R > 0.95

**Trigonometric Series Derivation**:
When Q/K are concentrated (qf ≈ q̄f, kf ≈ k̄f), the RoPE attention logit reduces to:

logit(Δ) ≈ Σf ∥q̄f∥∥k̄f∥cos(ωfΔ + φ̄f) = Σf [af cos(ωfΔ) + bf sin(ωfΔ)]

This is a trigonometric series in Q-K distance Δ, where coefficients af, bf are determined by Q/K centers. The centers determine which distances receive higher attention via this predictable curve.

**TriAttention Scoring Function**:
Two components combined with adaptive weighting:

1. **Trigonometric Series Score** (Strig): Uses Q center to evaluate distance preferences
   - S_trig(k, Δ) = Σf ∥E[qf]∥ · ∥kf∥ · cos(ωfΔ + φf)
   - E[qf] is the Q center in frequency band f (from calibration)
   - φf = arg(E[qf]) − arg(kf) is angular difference between Q center and current key

2. **Norm-Based Score** (Snorm): Complementary signal for less concentrated heads
   - S_norm(k) = Σf (1 − Rf) · E[∥qf∥] · ∥kf∥
   - Weight by (1 − Rf) ensures norm contributes more when concentration is lower

**Final Combined Score**: S(k, Δ) = S_trig(k, Δ) + S_norm(k)

**Multi-Offset Averaging**: Since a key may be queried from any future position, score is averaged over multiple offsets D = {1, 2, 4, ..., 2^16}: S̃(k) = (1/|D|) Σδ∈D S(k, Δ + δ)

**KV Cache Pruning**:
- Window-based: Pruning triggered once every β = 128 generated tokens
- GQA support: Normalize-then-aggregate across query heads, retain key if any head deems it important
- Retain only top-B keys by final score

## Results

**Mathematical Reasoning Benchmarks**:

| Method | AIME24 | AIME25 | MATH 500 (512 budget) |
|--------|--------|--------|----------------------|
| Full Attention | 57.1 | 40.8 | 69.6 |
| SnapKV | 34.6 | 20.0 | 49.2 |
| R-KV | 25.4 | 17.5 | 46.4 |
| **TriAttention** | **42.1** | **32.9** | **56.0** |

**At Equivalent Accuracy to Full Attention**:
- On AIME25: 2.5× higher throughput (563.5 vs 222.8 tokens/s) or 10.7× KV memory reduction
- On MATH 500: 6.3× higher throughput (1405.2 vs 222.8 tokens/s) at 68.4% vs 69.6%

**Budget Comparison with R-KV**:
| Setting | R-KV | TriAttention |
|---------|------|--------------|
| Comparable Accuracy | 2048 budget, 760.4 tok/s | 1024 budget, 1405.2 tok/s (85% faster) |
| Comparable Memory | 1024 budget, 60.4% MATH | 1024 budget, 68.4% MATH (+8.0) |

**Memory Retention Benchmark (Recursive DFS)**:
- Up to depth 16, TriAttention performs comparably to Full Attention (slightly outperforms at depths 8 and 12)
- R-KV exhibits catastrophic degradation at depth 16: drops from ~61% at depth 14 to 31% at depth 16
- Confirms TriAttention preserves essential information for memory retention while R-KV's pruning incorrectly discards critical states

**Ablation Studies**:
- Without Strig: AIME24 drops from 42.1% to 18.8% (confirms trigonometric series essential)
- Without concentration weighting (R): AIME25 drops from 32.9% to 28.7%
- Cross-domain calibration (Coding data for reasoning tasks): comparable accuracy (44.2% vs 42.1%), confirming generalization

**Cross-Architecture Validation**:
- GLM-4.7-Flash (MLA architecture): 96.6% of heads achieve R > 0.95 (vs 84.7% for GQA), indicating Q/K concentration is architecture-general and even more pronounced in MLA

## Limitations & Future Work

**Future Improvements**:
- Dedicated hardware-aware inference kernel optimized for trigonometric series computation and cache pruning
- Evaluation on broader domains: coding and agentic tasks beyond mathematical reasoning
- Refined compression strategies including head-specific budgets

**Cross-Domain Generalization**:
- Calibration sensitivity shows Q/K statistics are model-intrinsic properties, robust to choice of calibration data
- Performance stable across calibration sizes from 50k to 960k tokens (45.4–45.8%)
- Low-quality data (Google homepage HTML) achieves comparable results to high-quality chat data
