# Beyond Accuracy: Unveiling Inefficiency Patterns in Tool-Integrated Reasoning

**Published**: 2026-04-06 | **Added**: 2026-04-08 | **arXiv**: [2604.05404](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.05404)

## Summary

PTE (Prefill Token Equivalents) reveals that standard token-count metrics for evaluating Tool-Integrated Reasoning (TIR) efficiency are fundamentally misleading. The paper shows that inference latency is dominated by the asymmetry between prefill (compute-bound) and decode (memory-bound) phases, and that existing metrics miss this entirely. PTE achieves r=0.925 correlation with wall-clock latency (vs r=-0.375 for raw token counts), revealing 4 specific inefficiency patterns with 1.77x-2.42x cost multipliers. The key finding is that higher PTE trajectories tend to have lower reasoning correctness—the effort spent on inefficient tool usage strategies actively harms performance.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces PTE (Prefill Token Equivalents), a hardware-aware metric modeling the physical costs of transformer inference including KV-cache eviction and long tool responses
- Identifies 4 specific inefficiency patterns in TIR: Confirmatory Tool Usage, Redundant Tool Usage, Tool-Mixing, and Superfluous Tool Usage
- Shows negative correlation between PTE and reasoning correctness, meaning inefficient strategies are counterproductive
- Validated across 5 TIR benchmarks and multiple hardware profiles

## Method

PTE quantifies total inference cost as: PTE = prefill_tokens + γ × cumulative_seq_len × decode_tokens, where γ is the ratio of decode-to-prefill cost per-token. The key insight is that decode tokens cost proportionally more as cumulative context grows (memory-bound regime), and tool-call pauses evict KV-cache entries causing recomputation. Four inefficiency patterns are identified: Confirmatory (using tools to verify own outputs), Redundant (same tool multiple times), Mixing (frequent alternation between tool and text), and Superfluous (unnecessary tool calls).

## Results

PTE achieves r=0.925 correlation with wall-clock latency vs r=-0.375 for token counts—a dramatic improvement in predictive power. The 4 inefficiency patterns show cost multipliers of 1.77x-2.42x. Trajectories with higher PTE tend to have lower reasoning correctness, suggesting that inefficient TIR strategies are not just costly but actively harmful to outcomes.

## Limitations & Future Work

PTE requires hardware profiling to determine γ. The 4 patterns are identified through qualitative analysis; a systematic pattern-mining approach could find additional inefficiencies.
