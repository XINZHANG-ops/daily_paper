# OmniShow: Unifying Multimodal Conditions for Human-Object Interaction Video Generation

**Published**: 2026-04-12 | **Added**: 2026-04-14 | **arXiv**: [2604.11804](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.11804)

## Summary

OmniShow addresses Human-Object Interaction Video Generation (HOIVG) by unifying four distinct multimodal conditions (text, reference images, audio, and pose) in an end-to-end framework. Built on Waver 1.0 (12B MMDiT-based latent diffusion model), it introduces three key innovations: Unified Channel-wise Conditioning, Gated Local-Context Attention, and Decoupled-Then-Joint Training.

The challenge is balancing controllability and generation quality while overcoming data scarcity through heterogeneous dataset utilization, with evaluation via the dedicated HOIVG-Bench benchmark.

## Key Contributions

- **Unified Channel-wise Conditioning**: Pseudo-frame tokens with channel concatenation for seamless injection
- **Gated Local-Context Attention**: Audio context packing with masked cross-attention for temporal alignment
- **Decoupled-Then-Joint Training**: Train R2V and A2V separately, then merge for joint fine-tuning
- **HOIVG-Bench**: 135 curated samples for evaluation

## Method

Uses pseudo-frame tokens and channel concatenation for unified channel-wise conditioning. Gated Local-Context Attention with audio context packing and masked cross-attention for temporal alignment. Multi-stage decoupled-then-joint training: separate R2V and A2V models first, then merge for joint fine-tuning with pose conditions.

## Results

- **Smallest 10B-scale model**: 12.3B parameters
- **State-of-the-art or highly competitive**: On R2V, RA2V, and RP2V settings
- **Superior reference consistency**: Audio-visual synchronization and video quality on HOIVG-Bench
