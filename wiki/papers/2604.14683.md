# DR3-Eval: Towards Realistic and Reproducible Deep Research Evaluation

**Published**: 2026-04-15 | **Added**: 2026-04-17 | **arXiv**: [2604.14683](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.14683)

## Summary

Deep Research Agents (DRAs) perform complex, long-horizon research tasks involving planning, retrieval, multimodal understanding, and citation-grounded report generation. DR3-Eval addresses the challenge of evaluating these agents in a way that balances realism, controllability, and reproducibility—responding to issues of dynamic web environments, ambiguous task definitions, and the gap between real-world research complexity and evaluation settings.

The framework combines authentic multimodal user files with a controlled static sandbox corpus, uses a reverse-construction methodology ensuring verifiable solution paths, and employs a multi-dimensional evaluation framework measuring Information Recall, Factual Accuracy, Citation Coverage, Instruction Following, and Depth Quality.

## Key Contributions

- **Five-stage pipeline**: Grounding in real-world needs, distilling search paths, building research sandbox, constructing queries, quality control
- **Multi-dimensional evaluation**: Five metrics covering information retrieval, factual correctness, citation quality, instruction adherence, and depth
- **Reverse-construction methodology**: Ensures each task has a verifiable solution path
- **Hallucination identification**: Reveals hallucination as the primary failure mode across all models

## Method

**Five-Stage Construction Pipeline:**
1. Grounding in real-world needs
2. Distilling search paths from expert research workflows
3. Building research sandbox with supportive documents, distractors, noise
4. Constructing queries from verified evidential documents
5. Quality control to ensure solvability

**Multi-Agent Architecture (DR3-Agent):**
- Hierarchical architecture for complex research tasks
- Separates planning, retrieval, synthesis, and citation

**Evaluation Metrics:**
- Information Recall: Coverage of relevant information
- Factual Accuracy: Correctness of stated facts
- Citation Coverage: Proper attribution to sources
- Instruction Following: Adherence to user requirements
- Depth Quality: Analysis depth and insight

## Results

| Finding | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Best model | Claude Sonnet 4 |
| Longer contexts | Lead to performance degradation |
| Instruction following ≠ factual accuracy | Better adherence doesn't guarantee correctness |
| Primary failure mode | Hallucination across all models |
| Domain variations | Significant performance differences across domains |

## Limitations & Future Work

- Static sandbox cannot fully replicate dynamic web research
- Hallucination remains unsolved even for frontier models
- Reverse construction may miss emergent research paths
