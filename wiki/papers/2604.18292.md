# Agent-World: Scaling Real-World Environment Synthesis for Evolving General Agent Intelligence

**Published**: 2026-04-19 | **Added**: 2026-04-21 | **arXiv**: [2604.18292](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.18292)

## Summary

Agent-World addresses two fundamental bottlenecks in general-purpose agent training: (1) scalable realism in environment synthesis—existing LLM-generated or limited open-source environments fail to capture real-world interaction logic, and (2) lack of continuous self-evolving training mechanisms to diagnose agent weaknesses and drive continual improvement. The framework consists of two tightly coupled components: Agentic Environment-Task Discovery, which autonomously mines topic-aligned databases and executable tools from real-world sources (1978 environments, 19822 tools), and Continuous Self-Evolving Agent Training, which combines multi-environment GRPO with a diagnostic arena that identifies capability gaps and drives targeted learning. Agent-World-8B and 14B consistently outperform baselines across 23 benchmarks, with scaling analysis showing positive correlation between environment count and performance (18.4%→38.5% improvement), and self-evolution rounds producing monotonic gains (+3.3% to +8.6% across benchmarks).

## Key Contributions

- Agent-World training arena with two tightly coupled components forming a closed-loop training process
- Agentic Environment-Task Discovery: 1978 real-world environments and 19822 executable tools mined from web sources
- Continuous Self-Evolving Agent Training: multi-environment GRPO with diagnostic arena for co-evolution of agent policies and environments
- Demonstrates scaling laws: more environments → better performance, more evolution rounds → monotonic improvement
- Achieves strong results across 23 agent benchmarks including λ2-Bench, BFCL-V4, and MCP-Mark

## Method

Agent-World uses a POMDP framework for multi-turn agentic interaction. The Environment-Task Discovery pipeline collects themes from MCP servers (~2.8K), tool documentations (~0.5K), and industrial PRDs (~0.2K). A deep-research agent then mines topic-aligned databases from the web and generates executable tools via cross-validation. Tasks are synthesized through two complementary strategies: graph-based (tool-call DAG with random walk for sequential dependencies) and programmatic (Python solution code with verification scripts for complex reasoning). The Self-Evolving Training uses multi-environment rollout with GRPO, where the environment ecosystem serves as a dynamic diagnostic arena—capability gaps are identified from error traces and targeted training data is generated for iterative improvement.

## Results

| Model | λ2-Bench | BFCL-V4 | MCP-Mark |
|-------|----------|---------|----------|
| Agent-World-8B | 61.8% | 51.4% | 8.9% |
| Agent-World-14B | 65.4% | 55.8% | 13.3% |

Scaling analysis shows environment diversity strongly correlates with performance (18.4%→38.5% average across subdomains as environments scale). Self-evolution rounds produce monotonic gains of +3.3% to +8.6% across benchmarks, confirming the co-evolution loop effectively improves both agent policies and environment quality.

## Limitations & Future Work

- The approach requires significant infrastructure for environment mining and tool verification
- Scaling to even more environments may face diminishing returns without corresponding increase in task diversity
- Future work could explore automated curriculum learning over the environment taxonomy
