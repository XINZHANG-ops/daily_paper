## Summary

This paper introduces **process-driven image generation**, a multi-step paradigm that decomposes image synthesis into an interleaved reasoning trajectory of thoughts and actions. Unlike traditional single-pass image generation where a model commits to an entire scene in one forward pass, this approach unfolds across multiple iterations, each consisting of four stages: **Plan**, **Sketch**, **Inspect**, and **Refine**. The core insight is that humans paint images incrementally—planning layouts, sketching drafts, inspecting for errors, and refining details—and this process-oriented approach can be taught to unified multimodal foundation models.

The key innovation is the tight coupling between textual and visual reasoning. The textual reasoning explicitly conditions how the visual state should evolve, while the generated visual intermediate constrains and grounds the next round of textual reasoning. For example, when generating an image of "a bear hovering above a silver spoon," the model first generates a textual instruction like `<ins> Add a silver spoon standing upright on a smooth surface </ins>` paired with a scene description, then synthesizes a partial visual draft, then inspects for conflicts between sketch and prompt, and finally refines if needed.

A central challenge addressed is supervising interleaved trajectories with ambiguous intermediate states. The paper proposes **scene-graph subsampling** for contradiction-free incremental instructions and **self-sampled critique traces** that learn from the model's own errors, rather than relying on external symbolic corrections.

## Key Contributions

1. **Four-Stage Process-Driven Generation**: Plan → Sketch → Inspect → Refine cycle that co-evolves textual and visual states through autoregressive generation of interleaved text and image tokens.

2. **Scene-Graph Subsampling**: Method to construct generation paths by representing prompts as scene graphs and subsampling subgraphs to derive logically ordered incremental step-level prompts that expand compositions without contradictions.

3. **Self-Sampled Critique Traces**: Training signal derived from the model's own generated error trajectories, identified by a VLM judge operating on sampled trajectories—shown to outperform symbolic corrections by +6%.

4. **Comprehensive Intermediate Reasoning Dataset**: 32K multi-turn generation samples, 15K instruction-intermediate conflict samples, and 15K image-instruction alignment samples for supervised fine-tuning.

5. **Efficient Training**: Achieves 8× reduction in training data and inference cost compared to PARM while outperforming the 12B FLUX.1-dev model.

## Method

### Framework Architecture

Given a unified multimodal model $P_\theta$ and input text prompt $T$, the model generates a trajectory of alternating textual reasoning steps $s^{(i)}$ and intermediate visual states $v^{(i)}$:

$$\{s^{(1)}, v^{(1)}, s^{(2)}, v^{(2)}, ..., v^{(k)}, s^{(k)}\} \rightarrow I$$

The generation follows a four-stage cycle:

**Stage I (Plan)**: The model interprets the prompt and accumulated context to produce an incremental instruction `<ins>...</ins>` and a global scene description `<des>...</des>`.

**Stage II (Sketch)**: Conditioned on the planned instruction, the model synthesizes an updated draft image reflecting the intended modification, wrapped between `<|vision_start|>` and `<|vision_end|>` tokens.

**Stage III (Inspect)**: The model inspects both the textual incremental instruction against the raw complete prompt and the produced draft against the planned instruction to identify inconsistencies.

**Stage IV (Refine)**: If discrepancies are found, the model emits a refinement signal `<refine>...</refine>` and generates a corrected visual update.

### Training Objectives

The model is trained with Cross-Entropy (CE) loss on textual segments and a Rectified Flow objective for image generation:

$$L_{text}^{CE} = -\sum_{t \in [1,i]} \log P_\theta(s_t | y_{<t}, T)$$

$$L_{image}^{MSE} = \mathbb{E}\left[ \left\| P_\theta(z_t^{(i)} | y_{<t}, T) - (z_0^{(i)} - z_1^{(i)}) \right\|^2 \right]$$

where $z_t^{(i)} = t \cdot z_0^{(i)} + (1-t) \cdot z_1^{(i)}$ is the linear interpolation between noise and final image in rectified flow training.

The total training objective combines both:
$$L_{total} = \lambda_{CE} \cdot L_{text}^{CE} + L_{image}^{MSE}$$

### Dataset Construction

**Multi-Turn Generation Subset (32K samples)**: Scene-graph based sampling ensures each step updates only a specific local region while preserving spatial and semantic coherence of the rest. Ground truth synthesized using Flux-Kontext, filtered with GPT. Augmented with GPT-rewritten instructions for attribute modification, swapping, and removal operations.

**Instruction-Intermediate Conflict Subset (15K samples)**: Self-sampling strategy where generated intermediate reasoning traces from the fine-tuned model are evaluated by GPT for consistency with the original prompt. For conflicts, textual analysis and corrective instructions are generated.

**Image-Instruction Alignment Subset (15K samples)**: Extended from Gen-Ref dataset with positive samples (image consistent with instruction) and negative samples (image misaligns with instruction), each with GPT-generated explanations or refinement instructions.

## Results

### GenEval Benchmark (Compositional Object Alignment)

| Model | Single Object | Two Objects | Counting | Colors | Position | Color Attr. | Overall |
|-------|--------------|-------------|----------|--------|----------|-------------|---------|
| FLUX.1-dev (12B) | 0.98 | 0.93 | 0.75 | 0.93 | 0.68 | 0.65 | 0.82 |
| BAGEL-7B | 0.99 | 0.95 | 0.76 | 0.87 | 0.51 | 0.56 | 0.77 |
| **Ours (BAGEL + Process-driven)** | **0.99** | **0.95** | **0.75** | **0.87** | **0.72** | **0.69** | **0.83** |

The method achieves comparable performance to the 12B FLUX.1-dev while using only 7B parameters, with particularly large gains on relational and attribute-sensitive tasks like position (+21%) and color attributes (+23%).

### WISE Benchmark (World Knowledge Reasoning)

| Model | Culture | Time | Biology | Physics | Chemistry | Overall |
|-------|---------|------|---------|---------|----------|---------|

Nearly 15% gains on challenging Time and Chemistry domains, demonstrating enhanced generalization to complex concepts through interleaved reasoning.

### Efficiency Comparison with PARM

| Method | Training Dataset | Inference Cost | GenEval |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|---------|
| PARM (TTS) | 400K | 1000 | 0.67 |

8× reduction in training data (62K vs 688K) and 7.6× reduction in inference cost (131 vs 1000 steps).

## Ablation Studies

- **Instruction diversity**: Augmenting additive prompts with richer operations (refine, remove, swap) boosts performance by +6% overall, with +9% on position and +12% on attributes.
- **Self-sampling vs symbolic corrections**: Self-sampled critiques yield +6% improvement over scene-graph-derived corrections, confirming that internalizing the model's own failure modes is more effective than imposing external fixes.
- **Complementary supervision**: Instruction-intermediate conflict supervision improves semantic/spatial consistency; Image-instruction alignment supervision improves visually grounding (Counting +12%).

## Limitations & Future Work

1. **Complexity-adaptive reasoning**: The model autonomously determines trajectory length based on task difficulty, but this may not always align with desired output quality.

2. **Scene graph dependency**: Quality of generated instructions depends on the quality of scene graph construction from prompts.

3. **Future extensions**: The paper mentions extending unified multimodal reasoning to videos and 3D space, and enabling real-time human-in-the-loop control.
