# Think in Strokes, Not Pixels: Process-Driven Image Generation via Interleaved Reasoning

**Published**: 2026-04-07 | **Added**: 2026-04-09 | **arXiv**: [2604.04746](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.04746)

## Summary

This paper introduces **process-driven image generation**, a paradigm that decomposes image synthesis into an interleaved reasoning trajectory of Plan → Sketch → Inspect → Refine stages. Unlike single-pass generation which commits to an entire scene in one forward pass, this approach allows the model to progressively construct images stroke by stroke, decision by decision, with each step grounded in evolving visual states.

The key insight is that complex spatial relationships and fine-grained visual details are inherently difficult to encode through one-shot paradigms. By interleaving textual and visual reasoning, the model can detect and correct intermediate errors (conflicts between step-level instruction and overall prompt, or mismatches between generated draft and instruction).

## Key Contributions

- **Process-driven paradigm**: Decomposes image generation into a co-evolving loop of textual planning and visual sketching with self-inspection and refinement
- **Scene-graph subsampling**: Ensures logically ordered incremental prompts without contradictions
- **Self-sampled critiques**: Learning from model's own error trajectories rather than external symbolic corrections
- **Unified multimodal backbone**: Uses BAGEL-7B to autoregressively generate interleaved text and image tokens

## Method

The model follows a four-stage cycle: (1) **Plan** produces incremental instruction and global scene description, (2) **Sketch** synthesizes a partial visual draft, (3) **Inspect** detects conflicts between sketch/plan/prompt, (4) **Refine** produces correction if discrepancies found. Training uses combined CE and MSE losses on three subsets: multi-turn generation, instruction-intermediate conflict reasoning, and image-instruction alignment reasoning.

## Results

- **GenEval**: BAGEL-7B improves from 79% to 83% (+4%)
- **WISE**: Improves from 70% to 76% (+6%)
- **vs PARM**: Achieves 0.83 vs 0.77 on GenEval with 8× reduction in training data and inference cost
- **Key insight**: Self-sampling (learning from model's own errors) outperforms symbolic corrections by +6%

## Personal Notes

The concept of "process-driven" generation mirrors how humans actually paint—incrementally planning, sketching, and refining. The finding that self-sampled critiques work better than external fixes is philosophically interesting: it suggests models internalize failure modes more effectively than following prescribed corrections.
