# Rethinking Generalization in Reasoning SFT: A Conditional Analysis on Optimization, Data, and Model Capability

**Published**: 2026-04-07 | **Added**: 2026-04-13 | **arXiv**: [2604.06628](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.06628)

## Summary

This paper challenges the prevailing notion that "SFT memorizes, RL generalizes" by demonstrating that cross-domain generalization in reasoning SFT is **conditional** rather than absent—it depends jointly on three factors: (1) optimization sufficiency (sufficient training epochs), (2) data quality and structure (verified long-CoT traces), and (3) base-model capability.

The key findings are: (1) cross-domain generalization emerges after extended training following a "dip-and-recovery" dynamic—short-epoch checkpoints underestimate gains; (2) verified long-CoT data with procedural reasoning patterns yields stronger transfer than unverified or no-CoT data; (3) higher-capability models internalize transferable reasoning patterns (backtracking, self-correction) more effectively; (4) the asymmetry: reasoning gains consistently accompany safety degradation.

## Key Contributions

- **Conditional generalization**: Cross-domain generalization in reasoning SFT depends on optimization, data quality, and model capability jointly
- **Dip-and-recovery dynamic**: Cross-domain performance first drops significantly during extended training, then gradually recovers to exceed base model levels
- **Verified long-CoT importance**: Data quality matters more than quantity for transfer
- **Safety-asymmetry**: Reasoning gains always come with safety degradation because procedural patterns enable workarounds of safety guardrails

## Method

Systematic experiments varying: (1) optimization (training duration, learning rates, schedules), (2) data (long-CoT vs no-CoT, verified vs unverified), (3) model capability (1.7B to 14B). Evaluations span in-domain (MATH500, AIME24), OOD reasoning (LCB v2, GPQA-D), general capabilities (MMLU-Pro, IFEval), and safety (HEx-PHI).

## Results

- Extended training yields cross-domain gains that short checkpoints miss
- Verified long-CoT data with procedural patterns crucial for transfer
- Larger models internalize transferable patterns (backtracking, self-correction) while smaller models only imitate verbosity
- Safety degradation is an unavoidable cost of reasoning improvement
