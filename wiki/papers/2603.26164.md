## Summary

DataFlex addresses a critical fragmentation problem in the data-centric AI landscape. As large language models increasingly rely on massive heterogeneous corpora, simply scaling training data is insufficient—the quality, composition, and selection of data itself have become critical factors influencing model performance. DataFlex proposes a unified framework built upon LLaMA-Factory that treats data as a first-class optimization variable, providing unified interfaces for dynamically controlling which samples are selected, how different data sources are mixed, and how individual samples contribute to gradient updates during training.

The framework introduces a modular data-model interaction architecture with three trainer abstractions: Select Trainer for dynamic sample selection based on utility signals (e.g., gradient-based influence from LESS/NICE, loss-based scoring), Mix Trainer for online/offline domain mixture optimization (e.g., DoReMi, ODM), and Weight Trainer for dynamic per-sample reweighting during backpropagation. This design enables both online methods that adapt data decisions during training and offline methods that precompute strategies before training begins.

By building on LLaMA-Factory's mature infrastructure, DataFlex inherits native support for mixed-precision training, distributed data parallelism, and DeepSpeed ZeRO-3, while standardizing shared model-dependent operations like embedding extraction, model inference, and gradient computation. The framework achieves consistent MMLU improvements (LESS gains 5.8pp over static baseline on Mistral-7B), improves both MMLU accuracy and perplexity for data mixture methods, and delivers 3-7% runtime reductions compared to original implementations—with 57% speedup on 8 GPUs for LESS through distributed gradient collection.

## Key Contributions

- **Unified Data-Centric Paradigm**: Integrates three major paradigms—data selection, data mixture optimization, and data reweighting—under a single training framework, enabling systematic comparison of online and offline algorithms
- **Modular Trainer Architecture**: Replaces the LLaMA-Factory training layer with extensible abstractions (Select/Mix/Weight Trainers) and pluggable algorithm components, serving as a drop-in replacement for standard workflows
- **Distributed Gradient Collection**: Leverages DeepSpeed ZeRO-3 compatibility to reconstruct full gradients from partitioned shards via safe_get_full_grad, enabling gradient-based data selection at scale
- **Comprehensive Algorithm Support**: Implements 7 data selection algorithms (LESS, NICE, Loss, Delta Loss, NEAR, TSDS), 2 mixture methods (DoReMi, ODM), and 1 reweighting method under unified interfaces
- **Efficient Reimplementation**: Reduces runtime by 3-7% vs original codebases while maintaining or improving accuracy; achieves 57% speedup on 8 GPUs for LESS through parallel scalability

## Method

**Core Architecture**: DataFlex sits atop LLaMA-Factory as a drop-in replacement for the training layer, preserving model management, data processing, and optimization components while adding dynamic data control capabilities.

**Trainer Layer**:
- **Select Trainer**: Dynamically selects training samples using selectors that score/rank samples based on gradient-based influence (LESS estimates sample utility via gradient approximation), black-box optimization (NICE handles non-differentiable metrics), or loss-based signals
- **Mix Trainer**: Adjusts domain mixture ratios during training via mixers, supporting offline methods like DoReMi (minimax-optimized domain weights via proxy model) and online methods like ODM (multi-armed bandit exploration with Exp3 algorithm)
- **Weight Trainer**: Modulates per-sample training weights at each step using weighter components based on loss signals

**Training Scheduling**: All data-centric operations execute at configurable intervals (warmup_step, update_step) rather than every step, with caching of decisions for reuse and lightweight proxy signals when full gradients are unnecessary. Components are registered via decorators and auto-discovered at runtime.

**Distributed Support**: The safe_get_full_grad interface reconstructs full gradients from ZeRO-3 partitioned shards, enabling model-parallel data selection. Operations scale across multi-node, multi-GPU settings without centralized coordination.

## Results

**Data Selection on MMLU**:
| Method | Mistral-7B | Llama-3.2-3B |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| LESS | 45.2% (+5.8pp) | 45.0% (+13.1pp) |
| Reweight | 42.9% (+3.5pp) | 45.3% (+13.4pp) |
| NICE | 41.8% (+2.4pp) | 42.8% (+10.9pp) |
| TSDS | 42.9% (+3.5pp) | 34.5% (+2.6pp) |
| Static Baseline | 39.4% | 31.9% |

**Data Mixture on SlimPajama** (Qwen2.5-1.5B, 6B tokens):
| Method | MMLU | Perplexity |
|-------|------|----------|
| ODM | 26.04% | 4.244 |
| DoReMi | 25.84% | 4.134 |
| Baseline | 25.27% | 4.217 |

**Efficiency Gains**: DataFlex-LESS reduces training time by 3.7-7.1% vs original LESS; 8-GPU configuration achieves 57% reduction (12,965s vs 28,734s).

## Limitations & Future Work

- **Algorithmic Scope**: Current implementation focuses on instruction tuning and pretraining scenarios; extension to RLHF and other training paradigms requires additional abstraction
- **Model Dependencies**: Some data-centric methods require specific model architectures or training loops; further generalization of the component interface could broaden adoption
- **Scheduling Heuristics**: Warmup/update step scheduling remains task-dependent; automated scheduling strategies could reduce hyperparameter sensitivity
- **Benchmark Coverage**: While MMLU and perplexity are standard benchmarks, evaluation on more diverse downstream tasks would strengthen utility claims
