# Video-MME-v2: Towards the Next Stage in Benchmarks for Comprehensive Video Understanding

**Published**: 2026-04-05 | **Added**: 2026-04-08 | **arXiv**: [2604.05015](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.05015)

## Summary

Video-MME-v2 addresses a fundamental problem in video understanding benchmarks: inflated leaderboard scores that don't reflect real-world model capabilities. The benchmark introduces three key innovations—a tri-level capability hierarchy (information aggregation, temporal dynamics, complex reasoning), group-based consistency evaluation, and a non-linear scoring mechanism that penalizes fragmented correct answers while rewarding consistent performance. The dataset contains 800 videos and 3,200 questions with rigorous human annotation. Results reveal a massive gap between the best model (Gemini-3-Pro at 49.4 Non-Lin Score) and human experts (90.7), plus hierarchical error propagation from lower to higher reasoning levels.

## Key Contributions

- Progressive tri-level capability hierarchy spanning information aggregation, temporal dynamics, and complex reasoning
- Group-based evaluation assessing both capability consistency and reasoning coherence
- Non-linear scoring (quadratic rewards for consistency, first-error truncation for reasoning chains)
- First video MLLM benchmark to reveal systematic over-reliance on language priors

## Method

The benchmark was constructed through 3,300 human-hours of annotation by 12 annotators and 50 reviewers. Questions are grouped into consistency groups (related questions testing the same capability) and coherence groups (questions forming reasoning chains). The non-linear scoring mechanism uses quadratic rewards for consistent performance across groups and first-error truncation for reasoning chains—getting the first question wrong in a chain truncates all downstream credit. Multi-trial evaluation (k=3) reports Average Score, Pass@3, and Pass^3.

## Results

Gemini-3-Pro achieves 49.4 Non-Lin Score vs 90.7 for human experts—a 41-point gap. The benchmark reveals hierarchical bottlenecks where errors in lower capability levels (information aggregation) propagate to higher levels (complex reasoning). Thinking modes improve performance with subtitles but can degrade it in visual-only settings, indicating models rely heavily on language priors even when visual information is available.

## Limitations & Future Work

The 800-video scope limits diversity coverage. Future iterations should expand the video corpus and explore adversarial evaluation settings.
