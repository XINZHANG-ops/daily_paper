# SkillClaw: Let Skills Evolve Collectively with Agentic Evolver

**Published**: 2026-04-08 | **Added**: 2026-04-10 | **arXiv**: [2604.08377](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.08377)

## Summary

SkillClaw solves the problem that existing LLM agent skills remain static after deployment—failure modes and solutions are repeatedly rediscovered by individual users without knowledge accumulating system-wide. The solution is an **agentic evolver** that autonomously analyzes cross-user trajectories and drives continuous skill refinement or creation.

The key insight is that the "signal" for skill evolution comes from cross-user and over-time interaction trajectories, not from manual annotation or predefined templates. Skills evolve collectively, with successful patterns propagated across all agents.

## Key Contributions

- **Agentic evolver**: LLM agent with structured harness that diagnoses success/failure patterns and selects evolution actions (refine, create, skip)
- **Cross-user trajectory aggregation**: Aggregate interaction trajectories into shared evidence base
- **Nighttime validation**: Candidate updates validated in real environments during offline periods
- **WildClawBench**: Comprehensive benchmark for evaluating skill evolution over time

## Method

SkillClaw aggregates interaction trajectories across users into shared evidence base. The agentic evolver diagnoses patterns and selects actions: refine (improve existing skill), create (new skill for uncovered capability), or skip (no action needed). Validated during nighttime, synchronized back to all agents.

## Results

6 days, 8 concurrent users on WildClawBench with Qwen3-Max:
- Social Interaction: +11.72%
- Search & Retrieval: +52.00%
- Creative Synthesis: +88.41%
- Safety & Alignment: +33.33%

Demonstrates effective collective skill evolution where learning compounds across users.
