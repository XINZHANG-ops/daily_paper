# RAD-2: Scaling Reinforcement Learning in a Generator-Discriminator Framework

**Published**: 2026-04-15 | **Added**: 2026-04-20 | **arXiv**: [2604.15308](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.15308)

## Summary

RAD-2 addresses the instability of diffusion-based motion planners trained purely with imitation learning — they suffer from stochastic instabilities and lack corrective negative feedback. The paper proposes a generator-discriminator framework where a diffusion generator produces diverse trajectory candidates while an RL-trained discriminator reranks them based on long-term outcomes. This decoupled design avoids directly applying sparse scalar rewards to the high-dimensional trajectory space, improving optimization stability. The system uses TC-GRPO for credit assignment, OGO for trajectory distribution refinement, and BEV-Warp for efficient closed-loop simulation. RAD-2 achieves a 56% reduction in collision rate compared to strong diffusion-based planners.

## Key Contributions

- **Generator-Discriminator Architecture**: Diffusion generator produces trajectory candidates; RL discriminator evaluates and reranks based on long-term driving quality
- **TC-GRPO (Temporally Consistent Group Relative Policy Optimization)**: Exploits temporal coherence to alleviate credit assignment problems in high-dimensional trajectory spaces
- **OGO (On-policy Generator Optimization)**: Converts closed-loop feedback into structured longitudinal optimization signals, shifting the generator toward high-reward trajectory manifolds
- **BEV-Warp**: High-throughput simulation environment using spatial warping in BEV feature space — enables scalable RL training without expensive image-level rendering

## Method

**Generator-Discriminator Framework**:
- Generator G_θ(τ|o): DiT-based diffusion decoder producing M candidate trajectories
- Discriminator D_φ(τ|o, C): Transformer encoder scoring each candidate's long-term quality
- Joint policy: Π_{θ,φ}(τ|o) = E_{C~G_θ(·|o)}[D_φ(τ|o,C)]

**TC-GRPO for Discriminator Training**:
- Uses latched execution strategy: selected trajectory reused over fixed horizon for behavioral coherence
- Structured sampling ensures temporal dependencies across consecutive decision steps
- Group-relative advantage computation with adaptive entropy regularization

**OGO for Generator Refinement**:
- Safety-driven deceleration: when TTC falls below threshold, apply temporal compression
- Efficiency-driven acceleration: when ego vehicle lags behind reference, apply temporal extension
- On-policy dataset aggregation for generator fine-tuning via MSE loss

**BEV-Warp Simulation**:
- Warp matrix M_{t+1} = (P_{t+1})^{-1} P^{ref}_{t+1} derived from pose deviation
- Synthesized BEV feature via bilinear interpolation — bypasses image-level rendering
- Spatial equivariance preserved: geometric transformations in feature space correspond to physical movements

## Results

**Closed-loop performance in BEV-Warp** (512 safety-oriented + 512 efficiency-oriented scenarios):

| Method | Collision Rate (CR) | At-Fault CR | Safety@1 | Safety@2 | EP@1.0 |
|--------|---------------------|-------------|----------|---------|--------|
| TransFuser | 0.563 | 0.275 | 0.400 | 0.346 | 0.244 |
| VAD | 0.594 | 0.299 | 0.371 | 0.312 | 0.252 |
| GenAD | 0.592 | 0.305 | 0.363 | 0.309 | 0.467 |
| ResAD | 0.533 | 0.264 | 0.418 | 0.281 | 0.516 |
| **RAD-2** | **0.234** | **0.092** | **0.730** | **0.596** | **0.736** |

RAD-2 reduces collision rate by 56% compared to ResAD (0.533 → 0.234).

**3DGS Environment Results** (1,044 training + 256 evaluation clips):
- RAD-2 achieves lowest CR (0.250) among all methods
- Senna-2 (0.269), RAD (0.281), prior best

**Key Findings**:
1. Joint optimization (generator + discriminator) outperforms two-stage training
2. Reward-variance-based clip filtering stabilizes training
3. Group size of 4 optimal for TC-GRPO
4. Entropy regularization prevents score collapse
