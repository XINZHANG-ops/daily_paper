# LPM 1.0: Video-based Character Performance Model

**Published**: 2026-04-08 | **Added**: 2026-04-13 | **arXiv**: [2604.07823](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.07823)

## Summary

LPM 1.0 addresses the "performance trilemma" in video-based character animation: simultaneously achieving expressive quality, real-time inference, and long-horizon identity stability. The paper introduces a full-stack framework spanning a curated multimodal dataset, a 17B-parameter Diffusion Transformer base model (Base LPM), and a distilled causal streaming generator (Online LPM) for real-time deployment. The key insight is that conversation is the most comprehensive performance scenario—characters must simultaneously speak, listen, react, and emote while maintaining identity over time.

The system builds on three core innovations: (1) a large-scale human-centric multimodal dataset with speaking-listening audio-video pairing and identity-aware multi-reference extraction, (2) a 17B Base LPM trained on over 1.7 trillion multimodal tokens with interleaved speak/listen audio injection, and (3) a four-stage autoregressive distillation curriculum that converts the base model into Online LPM—a causal backbone-refiner architecture for low-latency, infinite-length synthesis. Human evaluations show Base LPM (720P) is preferred over Kling-Avatar-2 (64.3%) and OmniHuman-1.5 (42.5%), while Online LPM (480P) is preferred over LiveAvatar (82.5%) and SoulX (64.1%).

## Key Contributions

- **Performance Trilemma Solution**: First video-generative system to jointly address expressiveness, real-time inference, and long-horizon stability for conversational character performance
- **Full-Duplex Conversational Modeling**: Introduces listening behavior modeling—previous systems focus only on speech-driven talking heads, neglecting the critical role of attentive gaze, reactive expressions, and turn-taking cues
- **17B Base LPM**: Bidirectional Diffusion Transformer with interleaved speak/listen audio cross-attention, jointly modeling speech-driven motion, listening behavior, text-conditioned control, and multi-reference identity preservation
- **Online LPM Distillation**: Four-stage autoregressive curriculum converting base model into a causal backbone-refiner streaming generator for real-time, infinite-length synthesis
- **LPM-Bench**: First benchmark for interactive character performance with 1,000 test cases across five scenarios

## Method

**Dataset Construction:**
The pipeline processes diverse long-form videos through four stages: (1) single-shot extraction via scene detection, (2) quality filtering removing post-processing artifacts/blur/overlays, (3) conversation detection with LR-ASD fine-tuned for three-state frame-level classification (speak/listen/idle), and (4) captioning with embedding generation. A semantic verification stage using fine-tuned Qwen3-Omni filters false positives, achieving 78.37 F1 (vs 70.47 for Gemini 2.5 Pro).

**Listening Behavior Annotation:**
Frame-level audio state labeling achieves 89.75% accuracy on Domain1 and 87.63% on Domain2 for speak-vs-listen classification. The pipeline constructs speaker-only and listener-only audio tracks from multi-person clips. Listener data curation balances emotion, expression, energy, and motion—with rebalancing for rare but training-critical behaviors like emotion contrast and expression oscillation.

**Identity-Aware References:**
Multi-granularity reference images address single-reference under-specification. The pipeline extracts frontal/side/profile views, different expressions, and varied lighting conditions to provide comprehensive identity specification for the model.

**Base LPM Architecture:**
Built on a 14B pretrained image-to-video foundation with 3B additional parameters in interleaved speak/listen audio cross-attention blocks. The model accepts character image, multi-reference identity images, speaking/listening audio streams, and text prompts for motion control. Cross-attention is organized so speaking audio conditions even-numbered layers while listening audio conditions odd-numbered layers.

**Online LPM Distillation:**
Four-stage autoregressive curriculum progressively transfers visual quality and behavioral richness:
1. Backbone-only training for temporal coherence
2. Refiner introduction for detail recovery
3. Joint training with causal constraints
4. Streaming optimization for real-time inference

The backbone maintains temporally coherent latent trajectories while the refiner recovers high-fidelity details, enabling identity, synchronization, and motion realism over unbounded horizons.

## Results

**Human Preference Evaluations (Base LPM 720P):**
- Preferred over Kling-Avatar-2: 64.3%
- Preferred over OmniHuman-1.5: 42.5%
- Largest margins on identity consistency and motion dynamics

**Human Preference Evaluations (Online LPM 480P):**
- Preferred over LiveAvatar: 82.5%
- Preferred over SoulX: 64.1%

**Base vs Online Model (480P matched resolution):**
Human raters judge Base LPM and Online LPM indistinguishable in 42-88% of cases across all evaluation dimensions, demonstrating that real-time causal generation need not sacrifice perceived realism.

**Dataset Scale:**
- Speaker clips: 8.5% retention (23M clips)
- Conversation & Listen clips: 2.02% retention (5M clips)
- Overall retention rate below 10% after aggressive quality filtering

## Limitations & Future Work

LPM 1.0 currently focuses on single-person full-duplex conversation; extending to multi-person scenes and broader performance scenarios remains future work. The system requires identity-aware reference images for best performance, which may limit zero-shot character generalization. The tradeoffs between visual resolution (720P base vs 480P online) and real-time constraints represent an ongoing balance that could be further optimized.
