# LPM 1.0: Video-based Character Performance Model

**Published**: 2026-04-08 | **Added**: 2026-04-13 | **arXiv**: [2604.07823](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.07823)

## Summary

LPM 1.0 addresses the **performance trilemma** in character animation: simultaneously achieving expressiveness, real-time inference, and long-horizon identity stability. The approach combines a large-scale multimodal dataset, a 17B-parameter Diffusion Transformer (Base LPM), and a distilled causal streaming generator (Online LPM) for real-time applications.

Key innovation is the interleaved speak/listen audio injection strategy where speaking audio is injected in even-numbered layers and listening audio in odd-numbered layers. The four-stage autoregressive curriculum with backbone-refiner architecture enables the online model to maintain quality while being fast enough for real-time interaction.

## Key Contributions

- **Performance trilemma solution**: Balancing expressiveness, real-time inference, and long-horizon identity stability
- **17B Base LPM**: Full-quality model achieving state-of-the-art results
- **Distilled Online LPM**: Real-time capable model (480P) with minimal quality loss
- **Interleaved audio injection**: Speaking in even layers, listening in odd layers for natural conversation

## Method

Builds curated multimodal dataset with speaking-listening audio-video pairing and identity-aware multi-reference extraction. Trains 17B Base LPM with interleaved speak/listen audio injection and multi-identity conditioning. Distills into Online LPM using four-stage autoregressive curriculum with backbone-refiner architecture.

## Results

- **Base LPM (720P)**: Preferred over Kling-Avatar-2 (64.3%) and OmniHuman-1.5 (42.5%)
- **Online LPM (480P)**: Preferred over LiveAvatar (82.5%) and SoulX (64.1%)
- Maintains real-time inference and identity stability over extended durations
- Strong listening behavior modeling critical for natural human-agent interaction
