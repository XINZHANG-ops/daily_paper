# KnowRL: Boosting LLM Reasoning via Reinforcement Learning with Minimal-Sufficient Knowledge Guidance

**Published**: 2026-04-13 | **Added**: 2026-04-15 | **arXiv**: [2604.12627](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.12627)

## Summary

KnowRL addresses severe reward sparsity in RLVR by selecting minimal-sufficient knowledge guidance instead of using redundant, inconsistent, or computationally expensive hints. The key insight is treating hints as atomic knowledge points (KPs) rather than long solution prefixes or full templates, enabling more precise guidance.

The method uses **Constrained Subset Search (CSS)** to construct compact, interaction-aware KP subsets. A key finding is the **pruning interaction paradox**: removing one KP may help while removing multiple such KPs can hurt performance.

## Key Contributions

- **Minimal-sufficient guidance**: Decompose hints into atomic knowledge points for precise RL guidance
- **Constrained Subset Search (CSS)**: Construct compact, interaction-aware KP subsets
- **Pruning interaction paradox**: Removing one KP may help while removing multiple hurts
- **State-of-the-art at 1.5B scale**: New SOTA across eight reasoning benchmarks

## Method

KnowRL decomposes hints into atomic KPs, uses CSS to construct compact, interaction-aware KP subsets, and identifies pruning interaction paradox to optimize robust subset curation. The approach addresses the challenge that redundant or inconsistent hints can hurt training.

## Results

- **KnowRL-Nemotron-1.5B**: 70.08 average accuracy without KP hints (+9.63 over baseline)
- **With selected KPs**: 74.16 average accuracy
- **New SOTA at 1.5B scale**: Across eight reasoning benchmarks
