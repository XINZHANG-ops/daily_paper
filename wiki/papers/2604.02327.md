# Steerable Visual Representations

**Published**: 2026-04-01 | **Added**: 2026-04-06 | **arXiv**: [2604.02327](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.02327)

## Summary

SteerViT solves a fundamental tension in visual representation learning: traditional ViTs are frozen and unsteerable (no way to direct attention), while Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are steerable but lose visual representation quality because they are language-centric. SteerViT inserts lightweight gated cross-attention layers directly into frozen ViT blocks, conditioning them on text via a pretrained RoBERTa encoder. The approach achieves early fusion—visual and language representations intermix throughout the network—rather than late fusion where they only meet at the output. This creates a new Pareto frontier: steerability and representation quality simultaneously.

## Key Contributions

- First method to achieve steerability, representation quality preservation, and early vision-language fusion simultaneously
- Gated cross-attention mechanism inserted into frozen ViT blocks with only ~21M trainable parameters
- Referential segmentation pretext task trains vision-language alignment without degrading frozen representations
- New state-of-the-art on CORE retrieval (96% vs 44% for vanilla DINOv2) and competitive on MVTec AD anomaly detection

## Method

SteerViT takes a frozen pretrained ViT (DINOv2, MAE, or SigLIP) and interleaves gated cross-attention layers within each transformer block. Text is encoded by a frozen RoBERTa encoder. The gates learn to modulate how much each visual token is influenced by text. Training uses a referential segmentation task on 162k diverse images with 2.28M text-image pairs—given a text description and an image, the model must segment the referenced object. This task naturally encourages both precise localization (visual quality) and text grounding (steerability).

## Results

On CORE text-guided image retrieval, SteerViT achieves 96% accuracy (vs 44% for vanilla DINOv2 and 72% for CLIP-based methods). On MVTec AD anomaly detection, it reaches 82.1% PRO, matching dedicated anomaly detection methods without architectural modifications to the ViT. On personalized object discrimination, it outperforms fine-tuned DINOv2 (58.1% vs 48.0% PR-AUC).

## Limitations & Future Work

The method requires a frozen ViT pretrained on visual data, a pretrained language encoder, and a curated referential segmentation dataset. Scaling to truly open-world steerability with arbitrary natural language queries remains an open challenge.

- [[entities/steervit]] — SteerViT is the method itself—a gated cross-attention approach that achieves early fusion without fine-tuning the ViT, representing a new paradigm for lightweight vision-language integration.
- [[entities/mvtec-ad]] — MVTec AD is the anomaly detection benchmark where SteerViT matches dedicated methods; this is notable because anomaly detection was not the training objective but emerges from the referential segmentation pretext.
- [[topics/computer-vision]] — SteerViT contributes to the ongoing work of reconciling frozen pretrained models with task-specific control, a theme that runs through many computer-vision papers in this period.
