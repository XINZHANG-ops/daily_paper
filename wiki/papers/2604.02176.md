# Adam's Law: Textual Frequency Law on Large Language Models

**Published**: 2026-04-01 | **Added**: 2026-04-09 | **arXiv**: [2604.02176](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.02176)

## Summary

This paper introduces the **Textual Frequency Law (TFL)**, a fundamental insight that high-frequency textual data should be preferred over low-frequency data for both prompting and fine-tuning LLMs, assuming meaning is preserved. The law is grounded in the observation that higher-frequency data occurs more frequently in pre-training and is therefore easier for LLMs to understand. The paper proposes three components: **Textual Frequency Distillation (TFD)** to enhance frequency estimation via story completion, and **Curriculum Textual Frequency Training (CTFT)** which fine-tunes LLMs in increasing order of sentence-level frequency.

The key insight is that word frequency follows Zipf's law, and predicting rare (less frequent) words is a harder task than predicting frequent words. This explains why different paraphrases with the same meaning yield different performance—LLMs effectively "understand" high-frequency expressions better.

## Key Contributions

- **Textual Frequency Law (TFL)**: When meaning is preserved, high-frequency textual data should be preferred for both prompting and fine-tuning
- **Textual Frequency Distillation (TFD)**: Using LLMs to generate story completions that enhance frequency estimation
- **Curriculum Textual Frequency Training (CTFT)**: Training in increasing order of sentence-level frequency yields better results
- **TextualFrequencyPairedDataset (TFPD)**: A curated dataset with high/low frequency pairs for math reasoning, translation, commonsense reasoning, and tool calling

## Method

The framework uses word-level frequency multiplication to estimate sentence-level frequency: `sfreq(x) = ∏ₖ wfreq(xₖ)^(1/K)`. For TFD, LLMs conduct story completion to generate data that refines the original frequency estimation. CTFT arranges training data in order from low to high frequency for each epoch.

## Results

- **Math reasoning**: Up to 8% accuracy improvement on GSM8K with high-frequency prompts
- **Machine translation**: 99/100 language pairs showed improvement on DeepSeek-V3 using high-frequency prompts
- **CTFT fine-tuning**: Up to 30% BLEU improvement on translation tasks
- **Tool calling**: High-frequency partition consistently outperforms low-frequency across all tested models
