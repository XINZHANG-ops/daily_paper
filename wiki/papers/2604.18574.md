# When Can LLMs Learn to Reason with Weak Supervision?

**Published**: 2026-04-19 | **Added**: 2026-04-21 | **arXiv**: [2604.18574](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.18574)

## Summary

This paper systematically studies when RLVR (Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards) can generalize under weak supervision—scarce data, noisy rewards, and self-supervised proxy rewards—across model families (Qwen, Llama) and reasoning domains (MATH, SCIENCE, GRAPH). The key findings are: (1) generalization is governed by training reward saturation dynamics—models with strong domain-aligned pretraining exhibit prolonged pre-saturation phases and generalize, while others saturate rapidly and memorize; (2) reasoning faithfulness (whether intermediate steps logically support the final answer), not output diversity, predicts which models generalize; (3) SFT on explicit reasoning traces combined with continual pre-training on domain data enables generalization in models that previously failed.

## Key Contributions

- Training reward saturation dynamics as the key factor governing RLVR generalization
- Reasoning faithfulness (not output diversity) as the predictor of generalization vs memorization
- CPT + Thinking SFT enables Llama to generalize under weak supervision where it previously failed
- Systematic empirical study across diverse model families and domains

## Method

**Three weak supervision settings**:
1. **Scarce data**: Training on 8 to 2048 examples
2. **Noisy rewards**: Random or incorrect rewards at varying corruption levels
3. **Self-supervised proxy rewards**: Self-certainty, entropy minimization, majority voting

**Key analysis**:
- Training reward saturation dynamics distinguish generalization from memorization
- Pre-saturation phase: training reward climbs, model learns transferable patterns
- Post-saturation phase: further training yields diminishing returns

**Intervention for failing models**:
- Thinking SFT: SFT on explicit reasoning traces (not just final answers)
- Continual Pre-Training (CPT): Domain-specific pretraining

## Results

- **Qwen models** (strong domain-aligned pretraining): Generalize under all three weak supervision settings
- **Llama models** (no domain-aligned pretraining): Fail due to rapid saturation and memorization
- **Qwen tolerates** up to 70% reward corruption; Llama degrades at 50%
- **CPT + Thinking SFT** enables Llama3.2-3B-Base to generalize in settings where it previously failed
- **Reasoning faithfulness** (not output diversity) distinguishes successful models

## Limitations & Future Work

- Understanding what determines reasoning faithfulness at a mechanistic level
- Whether CPT requirements can be reduced through better reasoning trace design
