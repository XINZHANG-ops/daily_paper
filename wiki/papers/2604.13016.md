# Rethinking On-Policy Distillation of Large Language Models: Phenomenology, Mechanism, and Recipe

**Published**: 2026-04-13 | **Added**: 2026-04-15 | **arXiv**: [2604.13016](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.13016)

## Summary

This paper investigates why on-policy distillation (OPD) often fails or yields limited gains even with a stronger teacher, identifying two key conditions: **thinking-pattern consistency** and **genuinely new knowledge** beyond what the student has seen. The core mechanism is **progressive alignment on high-probability overlap tokens**, which carry 97-99% of probability mass.

Key findings: (1) successful OPD shows steady overlap-token alignment, (2) optimizing only overlap tokens suffices, (3) reward quality degrades on long trajectories. Two recovery strategies are proposed: **off-policy cold start** and **teacher-aligned prompt selection**.

## Key Contributions

- **Two conditions for OPD success**: Thinking-pattern consistency and genuinely new knowledge beyond student training
- **Overlap-token mechanism**: Progressive alignment on high-probability overlap tokens carries 97-99% of probability mass
- **Two recovery strategies**: Off-policy cold start and teacher-aligned prompt selection
- **Reward quality degradation**: OPD reward quality degrades on long trajectories

## Method

Controlled experiments, reverse distillation, and token-level analyses (overlap ratio, entropy gap, overlap-token advantage). Identifies that OPD fails when thinking patterns mismatch or when teacher knowledge isn't genuinely new to student. Recovery strategies use off-policy cold start and teacher-aligned prompt selection.

## Results

- Successful OPD characterized by steady overlap-token alignment
- Optimizing only overlap tokens suffices for effective OPD
- Proposed strategies rescue failing OPD configurations
- OPD reward quality degrades on long trajectories
