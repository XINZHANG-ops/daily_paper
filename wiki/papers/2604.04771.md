# MinerU2.5-Pro: Pushing the Limits of Data-Centric Document Parsing at Scale

**Published**: 2026-04-05 | **Added**: 2026-04-07 | **arXiv**: [2604.04771](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.04771)

## Summary

MinerU2.5-Pro demonstrates that the bottleneck in document parsing is not model architecture but training data. Using the same 1.2B-parameter architecture as MinerU2.5, the Pro version achieves 95.69 on OmniDocBench v1.6 (2.71 points above baseline) by expanding training data from under 10M samples to 65.5M samples through a co-designed Data Engine. The Data Engine addresses a fundamental paradox: the hardest samples that matter most for model quality are precisely the ones most difficult to annotate reliably.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies training data coverage and annotation quality—not model architecture—as the primary bottleneck in document parsing
- Co-designs a Data Engine with three components: Diversity-and-Difficulty-Aware Sampling (DDAS), Cross-Model Consistency Verification (CMCV), and Judge-and-Refine annotation pipeline
- Expands from under 10M to 65.5M training samples with improved annotation quality
- Achieves state-of-the-art without any architectural modification, only data improvements

## Method

The Data Engine has three components working together. **DDAS** ensures the training distribution covers long-tail, difficult samples rather than just common patterns. **CMCV** measures agreement among heterogeneous models to assess sample difficulty and annotation reliability—a sample is considered well-annotated if diverse models agree on it. **Judge-and-Refine** uses a model-based judge to identify unreliable annotations and refine them. A three-stage progressive training strategy gradually exposes the model to harder samples.

## Results

On OmniDocBench v1.6 Full, MinerU2.5-Pro scores 95.69 (+2.71 over MinerU2.5 baseline). The Hard subset improves by 2.43 points. All improvements are achieved without any architectural changes.

## Limitations & Future Work

The Data Engine requires significant computational resources for multi-model agreement checks. The approach is specific to document parsing and may not directly transfer to other domains.
