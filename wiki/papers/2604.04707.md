# OpenWorldLib: A Unified Codebase and Definition of Advanced World Models

**Published**: 2026-04-05 | **Added**: 2026-04-07 | **arXiv**: [2604.04707](http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.04707)

## Summary

OpenWorldLib addresses the definitional and engineering fragmentation in world models research. The paper provides a clear, actionable definition—**a world model is a perception-centered model with interaction and long-term memory capabilities for understanding and predicting complex physical environments**—and a unified inference framework with six modular components. The key insight is that diverse tasks like video generation, 3D reconstruction, and Vision-Language-Action (VLA) have been inconsistently categorized as world models when only those with genuine perception-interaction-memory triplets qualify.

## Key Contributions

- First formal definition of a world model with three necessary and sufficient capabilities: perception, interaction, and long-term memory
- Unified modular framework (OpenWorldLib) with six components: Operator, Synthesis, Reasoning, Representation, Memory, and Pipeline
- Comprehensive survey and reorganization of prior work (Ha & Schmidhuber, Dreamer, V-JEPA, etc.) under the new definition
- Demonstration across interactive video generation, multimodal reasoning, 3D scene generation, and VLA tasks

## Method

OpenWorldLib's six components are: **Operator** (input validation and preprocessing), **Synthesis** (visual, audio, and action generation), **Reasoning** (multimodal understanding), **Representation** (3D reconstruction), **Memory** (long-term context), and **Pipeline** (unified orchestration). The framework is evaluated on AI2-THOR and LIBERO with models including π0, π0.5, and Cosmos.

## Results

OpenWorldLib demonstrates capabilities across four VLA-relevant task categories. The paper's primary contribution is definitional and organizational rather than benchmark-winning. Text-to-video generation without complex perceptual inputs is explicitly identified as NOT a true world model capability.

## Limitations & Future Work

The framework is primarily an organizational contribution rather than a new training method. Evaluating across diverse world model instantiations requires standardized benchmarks that the paper does not fully provide.
