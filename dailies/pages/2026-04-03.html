SKILL0: In-Context Agentic Reinforcement Learning for Skill Internalization

Published: 2026-04-01

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.02268

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The work presents **SKILL0**, a skill‑based prompting framework for an embodied agent operating in the ALFWorld environment and a parallel **Search‑based QA** system. It introduces a reusable **SkillBank** that stores domain‑specific skill patterns for both physical manipulation tasks and information‑retrieval tasks.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Prior embodied‑agent research relies on end‑to‑end models or hand‑crafted policy scripts. The new idea is to modularise expert‑level behaviours into a library of **skill primitives** (e.g., “Systematic Exploration”, “Immediate Acquisition”) that can be dynamically invoked via structured prompts. For QA, the approach borrows from decomposition‑then‑search pipelines and adds tight evidence‑bounded answering and two‑source cross‑checking.
3. ❓ Problem: - **ALFWorld:** The agent must locate, pick up, clean, heat, cool, or place objects while minimizing redundant actions and respecting environmental constraints (light, sink, microwave, fridge). - **Search‑QA:** Complex questions require multiple intermediate facts, but naive single‑query strategies often yield incomplete or hallucinated answers.
4. 🛠️ Methods: - **Prompt Templates** for each skill (ALFWorld and Search) that embed “principle → when‑to‑apply” rules. - **SkillBank** enumerates 13 representative skills, each with a concise actionable pattern and guidance on when to activate it (e.g., “Grab When Seen”, “Sink First for Cleaning”, “Decompose Then Search”). - **Two‑stage execution:** (1) Skill selection based on task state, (2) Prompt‑driven action or search query generation. - **Compression factor** for image data (set > 1.0) to balance visual fidelity and transmission cost.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: The skill library yields predictable, low‑step policies for ALFWorld (e.g., fewer than 10 actions per task) and reduces answer hallucination in QA by enforcing evidence‑bounded, two‑source verification. Quantitative gains are demonstrated through higher success rates and lower average action/query counts compared with monolithic baselines.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 本文提出 **SKILL0**，一种基于技能的提示框架，用于 ALFWorld 嵌入式任务环境以及并行的**搜索式问答**系统。核心是构建可复用的 **SkillBank**，其中存放针对物理操作和信息检索的技能模式。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 前期工作多依赖端到端模型或手工策略脚本。新思路是将专家级行为模块化为一套**技能原语**（如“系统化探索”“即时获取”等），通过结构化提示动态调用。问答部分则借鉴“先分解后搜索”流水线，并加入“证据约束答案”“双源交叉核对”等机制。
3. ❓ 问题: - **ALFWorld：** 智能体需在受限环境中完成对象定位、抓取、清洗、加热、冷却或放置等操作，同时避免冗余探索并遵守光照、水槽、微波炉、冰箱等约束。 - **搜索式问答：** 复杂问题往往需要多个中间事实，单次查询容易产生不完整或幻觉答案。
4. 🛠️ 方法: - 为每项技能（ALFWorld 与搜索）设计 **Prompt 模板**，模板中明确“原则 → 何时使用”。 - **SkillBank** 包含 13 条代表性技能，每条配有简洁的行为模式和触发条件（如“看到即抓取”“先清洁后放置”“先分解后搜索”等）。 - **两阶段执行**：① 根据任务状态选择技能；② 通过提示生成动作或搜索查询。 - 设定 **图像压缩系数**（> 1.0）以在视觉质量与传输成本之间取得平衡。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 技能库使 ALFWorld 任务平均步数降至 10 步以下，成功率显著提升；在问答任务中通过证据约束和双源核对大幅降低幻觉答案比例。实验表明，相比单一整体策略，基于 SKILL0‑SkillBank 的方案在成功率、动作/查询次数等指标上均有明显改进。

Generative World Renderer

Published: 2026-04-01

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.02329

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper focuses on generative world rendering using a large-scale dataset from AAA games to advance bidirectional rendering (inverse and forward rendering).
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Based on existing inverse/forward rendering methods and synthetic datasets, the paper introduces a novel large-scale game-derived dataset with synchronized G-buffers and a VLM-based evaluation protocol.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper addresses the domain gap caused by limited realism and temporal coherence in existing synthetic datasets, which bottlenecks scaling generative rendering to real-world scenarios.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The paper develops a dual-screen stitched capture method with ReShade-based graphics API interception to extract 4M synchronized RGB and G-buffer frames (depth, normals, albedo, metallic, roughness), along with a VLM-based evaluation protocol for assessing real-world performance.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Experiments show that fine-tuning on this dataset significantly improves inverse rendering (better depth, normals, albedo, metallic, roughness predictions) and enables superior cross-dataset generalization and controllable game editing.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 该论文专注于生成式世界渲染，使用来自3A游戏的大规模数据集来推进双向渲染（逆向渲染和前向渲染）。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 基于现有的逆向/前向渲染方法和合成数据集，该论文引入了一个来自游戏的新型大规模同步G-buffer数据集，并提出了基于VLM的评估协议。
3. ❓ 问题: 该论文解决了现有合成数据集在真实感和时间一致性方面的局限性，这种领域差距阻碍了生成式渲染在现实场景中的规模化应用。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 该论文开发了双屏拼接捕获方法，通过基于ReShade的图形API拦截来提取400万帧同步的RGB和G-buffer（深度、法线、反照率、金属度、粗糙度），并提出基于VLM的评估协议用于评估真实场景性能。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 实验表明，在此数据集上微调显著提升了逆向渲染性能（更好的深度、法线、反照率、金属度、粗糙度预测），并实现了卓越的跨数据集泛化能力和可控的游戏编辑效果。

CutClaw: Agentic Hours-Long Video Editing via Music Synchronization

Published: 2026-03-31

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.29664

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper addresses automated music-driven video editing for hours-long footage using multi-agent frameworks powered by Multimodal Language Models (MLLMs).
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Based on AI-assisted video editing, video temporal grounding, highlight detection, and multi-agent video systems; introduces a hierarchical bottom-up multimodal deconstruction approach with collaborative Playwriter, Editor, and Reviewer agents for music-anchored storytelling.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper solves the challenge of transforming hours-long untrimmed video into high-quality short videos that simultaneously achieve precise music synchronization, faithful instruction following, and visually appealing aesthetics while maintaining narrative coherence.
4. 🛠️ Methods: CutClaw employs a three-stage pipeline: (1) Bottom-Up Multimodal Footage Deconstruction that abstracts video/audio into semantic units, (2) Playwriter Agent that anchors visual narrative to musical structure, and (3) Editor and Reviewer Agents that collaboratively perform fine-grained segment selection and validation.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: CutClaw significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, achieving 77.6 visual quality score, 70.0 instruction follow score, and 86.5 AV harmony score, while securing 49.8% user preference votes for visual quality and 48.8% for human-likeness.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 该论文探讨的是利用多模态语言模型（MLLMs）驱动的多智能体框架，实现小时级视频素材的自动化音乐驱动剪辑。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 基于AI辅助视频编辑、视频时序定位、高光检测和多智能体视频生成研究；创新性地提出自底向上的层级化多模态素材解构方法，并设计由Playwriter、Editor和Reviewer智能体协作的以音乐为锚点的叙事规划流程。
3. ❓ 问题: 该论文解决的核心问题是如何将数小时的原始未剪辑视频自动转化为高质量短视频，使其同时满足音乐精准同步、忠实遵循用户指令以及视觉美感，并保持叙事的连贯性。
4. 🛠️ 方法: CutClaw采用三阶段流程：（1）自底向上的多模态素材解构，将视频/音频抽象为语义单元；（2）Playwriter智能体将视觉叙事锚定在音乐结构上；（3）Editor和Reviewer智能体协作完成细粒度片段选取与验证。
5. 📊 结果与评估: CutClaw显著优于现有最优方法，视觉质量得分达77.6，指令跟随得分达70.0，音视频和谐度得分达86.5；在用户偏好投票中分别获得49.8%的视觉质量偏好和48.8%的人类编辑相似度偏好。 ===
