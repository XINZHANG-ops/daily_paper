Paper 1

Beyond SFT-to-RL: Pre-alignment via Black-Box On-Policy Distillation for Multimodal RL

Published: 2026-04-30

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.28123

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper proposes PRISM, a three-stage post-training framework for large multimodal models that inserts an explicit distribution-alignment stage between SFT and RLVR, lying at the intersection of multimodal reasoning and reinforcement learning.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Building on the standard SFT-to-RLVR pipeline and on-policy distillation, the paper introduces a standalone intermediate alignment stage using black-box adversarial on-policy distillation with a Mixture-of-Experts discriminator that decouples perception and reasoning feedback.
3. ❓ Problem: It addresses the distributional drift introduced by supervised fine-tuning, particularly the heterogeneous drift patterns in visual perception and logical reasoning that compound during subsequent RL optimization in multimodal models.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The method employs a three-stage pipeline (SFT, distribution alignment via adversarial on-policy distillation with an MoE discriminator using dedicated perception and reasoning experts, and RLVR) trained on curated high-fidelity demonstrations without requiring teacher logits.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Experiments on Qwen3-VL-4B/8B across multiple RL algorithms and benchmarks show that PRISM consistently improves downstream RLVR performance by +4.4 and +6.0 average points respectively, while reducing token usage and validating that alignment repairs SFT-induced drift.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 该论文提出了PRISM，一种在监督微调（SFT）与强化学习（RLVR）之间插入显式分布对齐阶段的大型多模态模型三阶段后训练框架，属于多模态推理与强化学习交叉领域。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 基于标准SFT→RLVR流程和on-policy distillation研究，论文新提出将黑盒对抗式on-policy蒸馏作为独立的中间对齐阶段，并通过混合专家（MoE）判别器分离感知与推理反馈信号。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了SFT引入的分布漂移问题，特别是多模态模型中视觉感知与逻辑推理的异质性漂移在后续RL阶段被放大的难题。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 论文采用三阶段流水线（SFT、基于MoE判别器的对抗式on-policy分布对齐、RLVR），利用带有专用感知和推理专家的判别器在无教师logit的情况下修复策略分布。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 在Qwen3-VL-4B/8B上的实验表明，PRISM在多种RL算法和基准上持续提升下游RLVR性能，平均分别提高4.4和6.0个百分点，同时提高了token效率并验证了对齐阶段修复了SFT漂移。

Paper 3

PatRe: A Full-Stage Office Action and Rebuttal Generation Benchmark for Patent Examination

Published: 2026-05-04

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.03571

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper introduces PatRe, a benchmark for evaluating large language models on full-stage, multi-turn generation of patent Office Actions and applicant rebuttals in the intellectual property and legal AI domain. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building on prior discriminative classification and static extraction works (e.g., HUPD, PANORAMA), it proposes the first generative benchmark that models patent examination as a dynamic, adversarial, multi-turn interaction between examiners and applicants. 3. **❓ Problem:** It solves the limitation that existing benchmarks treat patent examination as static, one-shot tasks, failing to capture the interactive, iterative justification-and-response process inherent in real-world prosecution. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The authors compile 480 real-world patent cases with complete examination histories and formalize two tasks—Office Action generation (with oracle and retrieval-simulated settings) and rebuttal generation—evaluated via deterministic metrics and LLM-as-a-judge semantic auditing. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Results show proprietary LLMs outperform open-source models but still suffer from hyper-critical false-rejection bias and statutory reasoning instability in proactive examination, while all models perform markedly better at reactive rebuttal generation, indicating current LLMs remain insufficient as independent patent examination systems.

Today's Reading Tips 今日阅读推荐

I recommend starting with the PRISM paper, which offers a broadly applicable post-training framework that repairs the perception-reasoning drift limiting current multimodal models; this foundational improvement is precisely what agents in Claw-Eval-Live and patent systems in PatRe desperately need. Both benchmark papers share a shift from static, output-only evaluation toward dynamic, process-grounded assessment—Claw-Eval-Live via live workflow execution and PatRe via adversarial multi-turn prosecution—revealing severe capability gaps even in frontier models. Together, PRISM provides a training path forward while the benchmarks define the urgent evaluation frontiers.
建议首先阅读PRISM论文，它提出了一个通用的后训练框架，修复了限制当前多模态模型感知与推理的分布漂移问题，而这正是Claw-Eval-Live和PatRe基准测试中所暴露的智能体能力的核心瓶颈。后两篇论文的共同之处在于均从静态、输出导向的评估转向动态、过程驱动的真实能力检验——前者聚焦实时工作流执行，后者模拟对抗性多轮专利辩论——揭示了前沿模型在复杂推理与执行中的显著缺陷。三者结合，PRISM指明了训练方法的改进路径，而两项基准则定义了亟待突破的评估前沿。
