Self-Distilled Agentic Reinforcement Learning

Published: 2026-05-13

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15155

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents SDAR (Self-Distilled Agentic Reinforcement Learning) in the domain of reinforcement learning for post-training multi-turn large language model agents. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building on GRPO and On-Policy Self-Distillation (OPSD), it introduces a token-level gated auxiliary distillation objective that selectively trusts privileged teacher signals while keeping RL as the primary optimizer. 3. **❓ Problem:** It addresses the catastrophic instability and asymmetric trust issues that arise when naively applying OPSD to multi-turn agents, where privileged teacher rejections often stem from imperfect skill retrieval rather than genuine student mistakes. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** SDAR modulates the OPSD loss per token via a smooth sigmoid gate based on the teacher-student probability gap, strengthening positive teacher endorsements and softly attenuating negative rejections without altering the GRPO policy loss. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Evaluated on ALFWorld, WebShop, and Search-QA across Qwen2.5 and Qwen3 families, SDAR substantially outperforms GRPO and hybrid baselines, avoids the instability of naive GRPO+OPSD, and maintains gains even under random skill retrieval.

MemEye: A Visual-Centric Evaluation Framework for Multimodal Agent Memory

Published: 2026-05-13

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15128

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents a visual-centric evaluation framework and benchmark for long-term multimodal agent memory, focusing on whether agents preserve and reason over fine-grained visual evidence across extended interactions. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** It builds upon existing long-term memory benchmarks (e.g., LoCoMo, MMRC, Mem-Gallery) that are predominantly text-centric or allow textual shortcuts, and introduces a two-axis taxonomy (visual evidence granularity from scene to pixel level, and memory reasoning depth from atomic retrieval to evolutionary synthesis) to isolate visual memory bottlenecks. 3. **❓ Problem:** It addresses the gap that current evaluations rarely verify whether multimodal agents retain irreplaceable visual details and can track evolving visual states over time, as most visually grounded questions can be answered via captions or dialogue text alone. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The authors propose the MemEye framework, construct a benchmark of 371 mirrored questions across eight life-scenario tasks with ablation-driven validation gates, and systematically evaluate 13 memory methods across four VLM backbones using LLM-as-a-Judge and exact-match metrics. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Results reveal that current architectures struggle to preserve fine-grained visual details and reason about state changes, showing a trade-off where text-based memory loses visual fidelity while image-based memory fails at temporal validity, suggesting future systems must combine visual evidence preservation with temporally-aware evidence selection.

SANA-WM: Efficient Minute-Scale World Modeling with Hybrid Linear Diffusion Transformer

Published: 2026-05-13

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15178

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper introduces SANA-WM, a 2.6B-parameter open-source world model in computer vision and embodied AI for efficient, camera-controllable, minute-scale 720p video generation.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: It builds upon SANA-Video, LTX2, and Gated DeltaNet, and newly proposes hybrid linear attention, dual-branch camera control, and a two-stage refinement pipeline to natively train long-horizon world models rather than distilling short-video generators.
3. ❓ Problem: It addresses the high costs of data, training compute, and multi-GPU inference in existing minute-scale world models, along with their insufficient precision in following camera trajectories.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The method integrates a high-compression LTX2 tokenizer, a hybrid GDN/softmax DiT backbone, dual-rate UCPE and Plücker camera conditioning, a long-video second-stage refiner, and a robust metric-scale pose annotation pipeline trained progressively from short clips to one minute.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Results on a new 60-second benchmark show SANA-WM achieves stronger action-following accuracy and comparable visual quality to large industrial baselines, completing training in 15 days on 64 H100s while enabling single-GPU inference with up to 36× higher throughput.
