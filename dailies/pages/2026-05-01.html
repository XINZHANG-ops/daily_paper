Paper 1

Heterogeneous Scientific Foundation Model Collaboration

Published: 2026-04-29

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.27351

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper addresses heterogeneous collaboration between language models and domain-specific scientific foundation models within agentic AI systems, spanning physical, life, and social sciences.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Based on prior work in LLM-based agents and multi-agent systems, the paper proposes Eywa, a framework that augments domain-specific foundation models with language-model-based reasoning interfaces (the "Tsaheylu" bond) to enable modality-native collaboration without forcing structured scientific data into language formats.
3. ❓ Problem: Language-centric agentic systems are fundamentally limited when handling scientific tasks with structured, non-linguistic data (time series, tabular data, equations) because serializing such data into language discards task-relevant information, creating a bottleneck that domain-specific models could otherwise solve.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The paper introduces a bidirectional FM-LLM communication interface ("Tsaheylu") comprising a query compiler and response adapter, implemented via the Model Context Protocol (MCP), and instantiates it into three progressively more sophisticated systems: EywaAgent (single-agent integration), EywaMAS (multi-agent composition), and EywaOrchestra (planning-based dynamic orchestration).
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Evaluated on EywaBench across nine scientific sub-domains, EywaAgent improves utility by ~7% while reducing token consumption by ~30% and execution time by ~10% compared to single LLM agents; EywaMAS and EywaOrchestra further improve performance while maintaining efficiency gains across all three scientific domains.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 该论文探讨了在智能体AI系统中，语言模型与领域特定的科学基础模型之间的异构协作，涵盖物理、生命和社会科学领域。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 基于先前在基于LLM的智能体和多智能体系统方面的研究，论文提出了Eywa框架，通过引入语言模型推理接口（"Tsaheylu"纽带），使领域特定的科学基础模型能够参与智能体协作，而无需将结构化科学数据强制转换为语言格式。
3. ❓ 问题: 以语言为中心的智能体系统面临根本性限制——当处理具有结构化、非语言数据（时间序列、表格、公式等）的科学任务时，将这些数据序列化为语言会丢失任务相关信息，形成瓶颈，而领域特定模型本可解决这些问题。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 论文引入了双向FM-LLM通信接口（"Tsaheylu"），包含查询编译器和响应适配器，通过模型上下文协议（MCP）实现，并将其扩展为三个逐步进阶的系统：EywaAgent（单智能体集成）、EywaMAS（多智能体组合）和EywaOrchestra（基于规划的动态编排）。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 在涵盖九个科学子领域的EywaBench基准上评估，EywaAgent相比单一LLM智能体提升约7%的效用，同时减少约30%的Token消耗和约10%的执行时间；EywaMAS和EywaOrchestra在所有三个科学领域进一步提升性能并保持效率优势。

ExoActor: Exocentric Video Generation as Generalizable Interactive Humanoid Control

Published: 2026-04-29

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.27711

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper addresses humanoid robot control using third-person (exocentric) video generation as a scalable interface for modeling interaction-rich behaviors between robots, environments, and objects.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: The paper builds on world action models, physics-based world model simulation, and generalizable humanoid control research. Its key new insight is leveraging large-scale video generation models' generalization capabilities by using third-person video as a unified interface that implicitly encodes coordinated interactions, effectively bridging high-level task instructions with low-level robot control without task-specific data collection.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper tackles the challenge of enabling humanoid robots to perform fluent interaction-rich behaviors that jointly capture spatial context, temporal dynamics, robot actions, and task intent—current systems struggle to generalize across diverse environments and tasks, and scaling typically requires expensive data collection or domain-specific tuning.
4. 🛠️ Methods: ExoActor employs a three-stage pipeline: (1) third-person video generation via robot-to-human embodiment transfer and task-to-action decomposition, (2) interaction-aware motion estimation using GENMO for whole-body and WiLoR for hand pose estimation, and (3) general motion tracking deployment via the SONIC controller to transform estimated motions into dynamically feasible robot behaviors.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: The implemented end-to-end system demonstrates feasibility across tasks of increasing difficulty (B-level navigation, A-level coarse interaction, S-level fine manipulation), achieving successful execution without task-specific data collection. Ablation studies validate Kling 3 as the optimal video generator, direct motion feeding over retargeting, and the importance of viewpoint selection for different task types, while latency analysis identifies video generation as the primary bottleneck.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 该论文聚焦于人形机器人控制，研究利用第三人称（外视角）视频生成作为可扩展接口，来建模机器人、环境与物体之间丰富的交互行为。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 论文基于世界动作模型、基于世界模型的物理仿真以及通用人形控制等研究。其核心创新在于利用大规模视频生成模型的泛化能力，将第三人称视频作为统一接口，隐式编码协调交互，从而无需特定任务数据采集即可桥接高层任务指令与底层机器人控制。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决的问题是如何让人形机器人执行流畅的交互丰富行为，同时捕捉空间上下文、时序动态、机器人动作和任务意图。当前系统难以跨不同环境和任务泛化，而扩展通常需要昂贵的数据收集或领域特定调优。
4. 🛠️ 方法: ExoActor采用三阶段流程：（1）通过机器人-人形化身转换和任务-动作分解进行第三人称视频生成；（2）使用GENMO进行全身运动估计、WiLoR进行手部姿态估计的交互感知运动估计；（3）通过SONIC控制器进行通用运动跟踪部署，将估计的运动转化为动力学可行的机器人行为。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 实现的端到端系统验证了在难度递增的任务上的可行性（B级导航、A级粗交互、S级精细操作），且无需特定任务数据采集即可成功执行。消融实验验证了Kling 3作为最优视频生成模型、直接运动输入优于重定向、以及视角选择对不同任务类型的重要性；延迟分析确定视频生成为主要瓶颈。 ===

RADIO-ViPE: Online Tightly Coupled Multi-Modal Fusion for Open-Vocabulary Semantic SLAM in Dynamic Environments

Published: 2026-04-27

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.26067

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper addresses online semantic SLAM for open‑vocabulary language grounding of 3D scenes from raw monocular RGB video in dynamic environments. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** It builds on ViPE and foundation models (RADIO) and introduces a tightly coupled vision‑language‑geometric fusion with a temporally consistent adaptive robust kernel for calibration‑free operation. 3. **❓ Problem:** The problem is the lack of an online, calibration‑free SLAM system that can perform open‑vocabulary semantic grounding in dynamic environments. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The approach extracts dense RADSeg embeddings, foundation depth, and optical flow to build a factor‑graph BA that jointly optimizes poses, depth, intrinsics, and semantic features, while a temporally‑stable adaptive robust kernel selects ℓ2, Huber, or Cauchy losses per pixel. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Evaluations on TUM‑RGBD and Replica demonstrate state‑of‑the‑art ATE in dynamic scenes and top‑3 open‑vocabulary segmentation performance, confirming the system’s robustness and efficiency.
