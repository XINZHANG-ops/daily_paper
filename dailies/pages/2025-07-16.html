MoVieS: Motion-Aware 4D Dynamic View Synthesis in One Second

Published: 2025-07-14

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2507.10065

1. üìò Topic and Domain: Feed-forward dynamic 3D scene reconstruction and novel view synthesis from monocular videos using motion-aware Gaussian primitives.
2. üí° Previous Research and New Ideas: Based on static scene reconstruction and 3D Gaussian Splatting methods, proposes novel "dynamic splatter pixels" that unify appearance, geometry and motion modeling in a single framework.
3. ‚ùì Problem: Existing methods treat 3D tasks in isolation, focus mainly on static scenes, and require costly per-scene optimization without learning prior knowledge.
4. üõ†Ô∏è Methods: Uses a transformer backbone to encode video frames and three specialized heads (depth, splatter, motion) to predict 3D Gaussian primitives and their temporal deformation, trained on diverse datasets.
5. üìä Results and Evaluation: Achieves competitive performance on novel view synthesis and 3D point tracking benchmarks while being orders of magnitude faster (0.93s vs 10-45min per scene), and enables zero-shot applications like scene flow estimation.
