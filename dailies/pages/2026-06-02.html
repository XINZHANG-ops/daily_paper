Paper 1

On the Scaling of PEFT: Towards Million Personal Models of Trillion Parameters

Published: 2026-05-31

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.02437

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper investigates parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT), specifically LoRA, as a scalable architecture for supporting millions of persistent personal models atop trillion-parameter shared foundation models by treating small adapters as persistent local adaptive state.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Building on prior work in LoRA, reinforcement learning fine-tuning, and agentic systems, the paper introduces a three-axis scaling framework (Scale Up, Scale Down, Scale Out) that reconceptualizes PEFT from a budget-saving alternative into a compact, persistent unit of individualized behavioral state at population scale.
3. ❓ Problem: It solves the problem of how small trainable adapters can serve as durable, instance-specific behavioral state—encoding preferences, skills, and memory-like updates—on powerful shared base models while remaining economically feasible to train, evaluate, serve, and compose for massive populations.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The authors employ coupled scaling studies across three axes: trillion-parameter MoE LoRA RL to strengthen shared priors, rank regime analysis with RL-native initialization (OLoRA-tail) and stateful adapters (δ-mem) to minimize local adaptive state, and memory capacity laws with diversity-based majority voting to enable population-scale coexistence, supported by the MinT infrastructure.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: The results show that trillion-scale LoRA RL is feasible at reduced compute, tiny adapters can be stabilized via OLoRA-tail and square-root hyperparameter scaling, LoRA memory capacity follows predictable laws, per-user adapters produce richer social simulation than shared-base agents, and diversity among LoRA variants improves AIME24 accuracy from 0.3644 to 0.4867 through majority voting.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 本文研究参数高效微调（PEFT），特别是LoRA，如何作为可扩展架构，通过在强大的共享基础模型之上将小型适配器视为持久的局部自适应状态，来支持数百万个持久化的个人模型。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 在现有LoRA、强化学习微调和智能体系统研究的基础上，论文提出了三轴扩展框架（Scale Up、Scale Down、Scale Out），将PEFT从节省预算的替代方案重新概念化为支持大规模人口的紧凑型持久个体行为状态单元。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了如何在保持经济可行的训练、评估、服务和组合成本的前提下，使小型可训练适配器作为承载偏好、技能和类记忆更新的持久化实例特定行为状态，运行于强大的共享基础模型之上的问题。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 作者通过三轴耦合扩展研究来探讨该问题：利用万亿参数MoE LoRA RL增强共享先验，通过秩范围分析与RL原生初始化（OLoRA-tail）及有状态适配器（δ-mem）最小化局部自适应状态，并借助记忆容量定律与基于多样性的多数投票实现群体级共存，所有研究由MinT基础设施支持。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 结果表明，万亿规模LoRA RL在降低计算成本下可行，微小适配器可通过OLoRA-tail和平方根超参数缩放实现稳定，LoRA记忆容量遵循可预测规律，每用户适配器比共享基础智能体产生更丰富的社会模拟结构，且LoRA变体间的多样性通过多数投票将AIME24准确率从0.3644提升至0.4867。

K-BrowseComp: A Web Browsing Agent Benchmark Grounded in Korean Contexts

Published: 2026-05-31

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.02404

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper introduces K-BrowseComp, a web browsing agent benchmark grounded in Korean contexts, comprising a 300-problem human-verified subset and a 100-problem synthetically generated adversarial subset.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: It extends prior English and Chinese browsing benchmarks (e.g., BrowseComp) and static Korean evaluations by proposing the first Korean-centric agentic browsing benchmark, along with a novel synthetic data generation method that targets identified failure modes to exploit the asymmetry between solving and creating browsing tasks.
3. ❓ Problem: It solves the lack of standardized Korean agentic benchmarks by providing a rigorous testbed to measure whether LLMs can retrieve and synthesize hard-to-find public information across Korean websites via multi-hop or parallel-branching reasoning.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The authors manually crafted and validated 300 problems requiring Korean web evidence, and generated 100 additional adversarial problems using a browsing LLM agent with hard few-shot exemplars and a failure-mode taxonomy, filtered for searchability, well-formedness, and adversarial difficulty.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Evaluated frontier LLMs achieved only 30.00–45.67% on the verified subset and 26.00% on the synthetic split, while Korean open-weight models scored 0.00–10.33%, with trajectory diagnostics showing failures primarily arise from inability to maintain candidates, constraints, and role bindings after retrieving relevant evidence.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 该论文提出了K-BrowseComp，一个根植于韩国语言与文化背景的网页浏览智能体评测基准，包含300道人工验证题目和100道合成对抗题目。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 论文在BrowseComp等现有浏览基准及静态韩语评测的基础上，首次提出针对韩语的智能体浏览基准，并创新性地利用基于失败模式分类法的对抗性合成生成方法来利用浏览任务中解题与出题之间的不对称性。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了韩国语智能体评测基准几乎空白的问题，为衡量大语言模型在韩语网络环境中通过多跳或并行分支推理检索并综合难以获取的公开信息提供了严格的评测平台。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 作者手动构建并验证了300道需基于韩语网络证据的多跳或并行分支推理题目，并采用受困难样本示例和失败模式分类法引导的浏览智能体生成100道额外对抗性题目，经可搜索性、良构性与对抗难度三重过滤后纳入数据集。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 结果显示，前沿大模型在验证集上准确率仅为30.00%–45.67%、在合成集上仅为26.00%，而韩国开源模型仅为0.00%–10.33%，轨迹分析表明模型失败主要源于在检索到相关证据后无法跨步骤维持候选实体、约束条件和角色绑定等状态。

VLMs are Good Teachers for Video Reasoning via Adaptive Test-Time Optimization

Published: 2026-05-31

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.02564

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper addresses rule-based video reasoning in generative AI, proposing a paradigm that leverages Vision-Language Models (VLMs) as teachers to guide Video Generation Models (VGMs) via adaptive test-time optimization. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building upon prior work on video reasoning with VGMs, test-time scaling, and VLM-as-Solver approaches, the paper introduces the "VLM-as-Teacher" paradigm where VLMs provide differentiable reward-based supervision for visual execution instead of textual plans. 3. **❓ Problem:** It solves the problem that VGMs inherently lack logical rule-following capabilities due to their visual fidelity optimization objective, and that existing VLM textual guidance suffers from a text-to-execution gap that cannot capture fine-grained spatiotemporal constraints. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The method employs a frozen VLM Teacher to synthesize task-specific process and goal reward queries, which are used to guide test-time online optimization of a lightweight LoRA module on a VGM Reasoner through differentiable VQA loss with efficient single-step latent prediction and lightweight surrogate decoding. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Evaluations on VBVR-Bench and RULER-Bench demonstrate that the proposed method achieves a 16.7-point average gain over the baseline, substantially outperforming Best-of-N scaling (+2.2 points) and VLM-as-Solver (+0.4 points) at comparable test-time cost.
