Paper 1

Scaling the Horizon, Not the Parameters: Reaching Trillion-Parameter Performance with a 35B Agent

Published: 2026-06-28

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30616

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: This paper focuses on long-horizon agentic large language models (LLMs), specifically exploring the scaling of agent capabilities (horizon) rather than model parameters within the broader field of AI agents and multi-step reasoning.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: The paper builds on existing efforts to improve long-horizon agents (e.g., GLM-5, Kimi K2.5, context-folding) and existing LLM frameworks (Qwen3.5-35B-A3B, DeepSeek-V4, GRPO, MLEvolve, Knowledge Graphs). Its new idea is to shift the scaling paradigm from "scaling parameters" to "scaling the agent horizon" through long-horizon trajectories and heterogeneous agent abilities, unified via a Long-Horizon Knowledge-Action Infrastructure and multi-teacher on-policy distillation.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper addresses two key bottlenecks in scaling agent horizons: (1) the lack of a unified infrastructure to connect external knowledge, actions, observations, and verifier outcomes for training long-horizon trajectories, and (2) the challenge of integrating heterogeneous and compositional agent abilities (e.g., search, tool use, MLE, scientific reasoning, instruction following) into a single unified model without conflicts between different reasoning patterns.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The paper proposes (1) a Long-Horizon Knowledge-Action Infrastructure built on a knowledge-action graph (KAG) with self-play proposer-solver-verifier expansion, (2) a three-stage training recipe consisting of full-domain supervised fine-tuning (SFT), domain-level teacher training via RL (GRPO) and science-enhanced SFT, and (3) multi-teacher domain-routed on-policy distillation (OPD) with salient vocabulary alignment (SVA) and domain-normalized aggregation.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Agents-A1 (35B MoE) achieves leading performance compared to 1T-parameter models (Kimi-K2.6 and DeepSeek-V4-pro) on SEAL-0 (56.4), IFBench (80.6), HiPhO (46.4), FrontierScience-Olympiad (79.0), and MolBench-Bind (56.8), and remains competitive on SciCode (44.3), HLE (47.6), BrowseComp (75.5), and GAIA (96.0); long-horizon case studies include a 12-hour MLE optimization run improving AUC from 0.58 to 0.9935 and an autonomous Earth science analysis of Cyclone Nargis.

2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 本文基于改进长时域智能体的现有研究（如GLM-5、Kimi K2.5、context-folding）以及现有LLM框架（Qwen3.5-35B-A3B、DeepSeek-V4、GRPO、MLEvolve、知识图谱）。其新思路是将扩展范式从"扩展参数"转向"扩展智能体视野"，通过长时域轨迹和异构智能体能力实现，并结合长时域知识-行动基础设施和多教师在策略蒸馏来统一模型。

Paper 2

Monte Carlo Energy Aggregation for Mobile 3D Gaussian Splatting

Published: 2026-06-28

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30017

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: This paper focuses on 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) for real-time novel view synthesis on resource-constrained mobile devices, specifically addressing the efficiency bottleneck in 3D scene representation and rendering for edge hardware.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: The paper builds on 3DGS (Kerbl et al.) and lightweight variants like Mobile-GS, Speedy-Splat, C3DGS, and LocoGS, which attempt to compress Gaussian primitives or reduce storage. The new idea is Flux-GS, which introduces a Monte Carlo Specular Energy Aggregator to compress high-order Spherical Harmonics (SH) into a low-order subspace without distillation, combined with a zero-cost SH enhancement module and a multi-view densification strategy.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper addresses two primary bottlenecks preventing 3DGS deployment on mobile platforms: (1) prohibitive storage and memory bandwidth from third-order Spherical Harmonics (48 coefficients per Gaussian), and (2) excessive redundant Gaussian primitives produced by single-view gradient-based densification, which causes overfitting and high inference latency.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The paper proposes three core techniques: (1) a Monte Carlo Specular Energy Aggregator that samples third-order radiance residuals via uniform spherical sampling and aggregates specular energy into a compact latent space, mapping it to first-order SH through an MLP; (2) an Attribute-Conditioned SH Enhancement module that predicts view-independent SH offsets using intrinsic Gaussian attributes and statically bakes them into parameters before inference, introducing zero computational overhead; and (3) a Multi-view Alpha-based Densification and Pruning strategy using stratified camera sampling and alpha-weighted error accumulation to ensure multi-view consistency. A WebGL-based cross-platform rendering framework is also introduced.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: On the Mip-NeRF 360, Tanks & Temples, and Deep Blending datasets, Flux-GS achieves competitive PSNR (e.g., 30.22 indoor, 24.45 outdoor on Mip-NeRF 360) while reducing storage to 1.6-4.6 MB and delivering 132-158 FPS on Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 mobile GPU—outperforming Mobile-GS in speed. Training time is drastically reduced to ~11 minutes compared to 64-136 minutes for baselines. Ablation studies confirm each component's contribution, and a user study shows a clear preference (43-60%) for Flux-GS's rendering quality.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 本文聚焦于面向资源受限移动设备的三维高斯泼溅（3DGS）实时新视角合成技术，具体研究3D场景表示与渲染在边缘硬件上的效率瓶颈问题。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 本文基于3DGS（Kerbl等人）以及Mobile-GS、Speedy-Splat、C3DGS和LocoGS等轻量化变体，这些方法尝试压缩高斯原始数据或减少存储。新思路是提出Flux-GS，引入蒙特卡洛镜面能量聚合器（无需蒸馏即可将高阶球谐函数压缩到低阶子空间），结合零推理成本的SH增强模块和多视角稠密化策略。
3. ❓ 问题: 本文解决阻碍3DGS在移动平台部署的两个主要瓶颈：（1）三阶球谐函数（每个高斯48个系数）带来的过高存储和内存带宽消耗；（2）单视角梯度稠密化策略产生的大量冗余高斯原始数据，导致过拟合和高推理延迟。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 本文提出三项核心技术：（1）蒙特卡洛镜面能量聚合器，通过均匀球面采样三阶辐射残差，并将镜面能量聚合到紧凑的潜在空间，通过MLP映射到一阶SH；（2）属性条件化SH增强模块，利用高斯固有属性预测与视角无关的SH偏移量，并在推理前静态烘焙到参数中，引入零计算开销；（3）基于多视角Alpha的稠密化与剪枝策略，采用分层相机采样和Alpha加权误差累积以确保多视角一致性。还引入了基于WebGL的跨平台渲染框架。

Paper 3

TACO: Tool-Augmented Credit Optimization for Agentic Tool Use

Published: 2026-06-28

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30251

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: This paper focuses on reinforcement learning for agentic multimodal models, specifically code-tool visual agents that perform visual question answering by executing Python code to manipulate images and reason over the returned views.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: The paper builds on GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization) and prior code-tool visual agents such as Thyme, DeepEyes, Pixel-Reasoner, and CodeV, as well as process reward methods like MIG, PACR, and SPAE. Its new idea is TACO, which couples a Differential Answer-Probe Reward (DAPR) with Outcome-Gated Advantage Routing (OGAR) into a single GRPO objective to provide judge-free, per-tool-call credit assignment that is robust to probe-hacking.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper addresses the inability of outcome-only rewards to distinguish useful, redundant, or misleading tool calls, and the limitations of existing process rewards that either fail to attribute final correctness to individual tool calls or require an external judge model for evaluation.
4. 🛠️ Methods: TACO introduces DAPR, a self-supervised reward that scores each tool call by the before/after difference of the agent's own answer-probe outcomes (positive for useful calls, negative for misleading ones, zero for inconsequential ones), and OGAR, a parameter-free token-level rule that routes the final-answer advantage only to outcome-responsible segments. Training follows a two-stage SFT cold-start then GRPO pipeline with gated dual-channel advantages and no KL penalty.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: TACO-7B achieves 68.1% average accuracy across 12 perception, reasoning, and general multimodal benchmarks, outperforming the best prior code-tool agent (PyVision at 63.7%) by 4.4 points, surpassing GPT-4o (58.5%) by 9.6 points, while also being the fastest with the lowest latency. Ablations confirm both DAPR (−4.5) and OGAR (−2.0) are necessary, and the method generalizes from Qwen2.5-VL-7B (60.4→72.0) to Qwen3-VL-8B (72.9→78.8).
1. 📘 主题与领域: 该论文聚焦于智能体多模态模型的强化学习，具体研究通过执行 Python 代码来操作图像并对返回视图进行推理的代码工具视觉智能体，应用于细粒度视觉问答任务。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 论文基于 GRPO（分组相对策略优化）以及 Thyme、DeepEyes、Pixel-Reasoner、CodeV 等先前的代码工具视觉智能体工作，并借鉴 MIG、PACR、SPAE 等过程奖励方法。其新思路是提出 TACO，将差分答案探针奖励（DAPR）与结果门控优势路由（OGAR）耦合到统一的 GRPO 目标中，实现无评判模型、抗探针攻击的逐次工具调用信用分配。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了仅基于最终答案的奖励无法区分有用、冗余或误导性的工具调用的问题，以及现有过程奖励要么无法将最终正确性归因于单个工具调用、要么需要外部评判模型进行评估的局限性。
4. 🛠️ 方法: TACO 提出了 DAPR（一种自监督奖励，通过智能体在工具调用前后自身答案探针结果的差异对每次调用进行评分，有用为正、误导为负、无影响为零）以及 OGAR（一种无参数的 token 级规则，仅将最终答案优势路由到对结果负责的片段）。训练采用两阶段 SFT 冷启动加 GRPO 流水线，使用门控双通道优势且无 KL 惩罚。
5. 📊 结果与评估: TACO-7B 在 12 个感知、推理和通用多模态基准上达到 68.1% 的平均准确率，比此前最佳代码工具智能体（PyVision 63.7%）高出 4.4 个百分点，超过 GPT-4o（58.5%）9.6 个百分点，同时具有最低延迟、速度最快。消融实验证明 DAPR（−4.5）和 OGAR（−2.0）均不可或缺，且该方法在 Qwen2.5-VL-7B（60.4→72.0）和 Qwen3-VL-8B（72.9→78.8）上均可泛化。

Today's Reading Tips 今日阅读推荐

Start with the Agents-A1 paper (Paper 1), as it tackles the broadest and most ambitious problem—scaling long-horizon agentic LLMs—and demonstrates striking efficiency by outperforming 1T-parameter models with just 35B MoE. The TACO paper (Paper 3) is a natural follow-up: both focus on agentic LLMs and share GRPO-based training, but TACO zooms in on a complementary bottleneck—per-tool-call credit assignment for multimodal code agents—offering techniques that could enrich long-horizon agent training. The Flux-GS paper is thematically independent and can be read later as a separate, self-contained contribution on mobile 3D rendering.
建议先读 Agents-A1（论文1），因为它解决的问题最具野心——扩展智能体大语言模型的长时程能力，并展示了仅用 35B MoE 模型就超越 1T 参数模型的惊人效率。TACO（论文3）是顺理成章的下一篇：两篇都聚焦智能体大模型且都采用 GRPO 训练，但 TACO 深入研究了一个互补的瓶颈——多模态代码智能体中按工具调用的信用分配，其方法可进一步丰富长时程智能体的训练。Flux-GS 在主题上相对独立，可作为独立的补充阅读，关注移动端三维渲染方向。
