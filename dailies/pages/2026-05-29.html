AgentDoG 1.5: A Lightweight and Scalable Alignment Framework for AI Agent Safety and Security

Published: 2026-05-27

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.29801

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents a lightweight and scalable alignment framework for AI agent safety and security, focusing on trajectory-level safety evaluation, fine-grained risk diagnosis, and online guardrails for open-world agents like OpenClaw and Codex.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Building upon the original AgentDoG and ATBench with a three-dimensional safety taxonomy, this work introduces an updated taxonomy for Codex/OpenClaw scenarios, a taxonomy-guided data engine with influence-function purification, and a training-free online guardrail system.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper addresses the inadequacy of current agent alignment frameworks in handling emerging broad safety risks from open-world agents (e.g., OpenClaw) and lowered adversarial attack barriers posed by advanced frontier AI models.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The authors propose a framework featuring an updated three-dimensional safety taxonomy, a taxonomy-guided data engine with influence-function-based data purification to train lightweight models (~1k samples), two-stage training (SFT + RL with GDPO), lightweight finite-state simulation environments, and deployment as a training-free online Pre-Reply guardrail.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: AgentDoG 1.5 achieves state-of-the-art or near-frontier performance across R-Judge and the ATBench family (including Claw and Codex variants) with models as small as 0.8B–4B parameters, effectively improves agent safety SFT/RL training, and reduces unsafe final-delivery rates when deployed as a real-time online guardrail.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 本文提出了一个面向OpenClaw和Codex等开放世界AI智能体安全与安全的轻量级可扩展对齐框架，涵盖轨迹级安全评估、细粒度风险诊断与在线护栏部署，属于AI安全与大模型智能体领域。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 该工作在AgentDoG和ATBench的三维安全分类法基础上，更新了面向Codex/OpenClaw场景的分类体系，提出了taxonomy-guided数据引擎、基于影响函数的数据净化方法以及无需训练的在线护栏部署新思路。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了当前智能体对齐框架难以应对开放世界智能体带来的新型广泛安全风险，以及前沿AI模型大幅降低对抗攻击门槛所导致的真实世界部署安全问题。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 研究采用更新后的三维安全分类法，结合taxonomy-guided数据引擎与影响函数数据净化筛选约1k样本，通过SFT和GDPO强化学习两阶段训练轻量级模型（0.8B–8B），并构建有限状态模拟环境与训练免费的Pre-Reply在线护栏。
5. 📊 结果与评估: AgentDoG 1.5在R-Judge及ATBench系列基准上取得SOTA或接近前沿闭源模型（如GPT-5.4）的性能，在SFT/RL训练中有效提升安全性并保留工具调用能力，作为在线护栏显著降低了不安全最终响应的交付率。

OmniRetrieval: Unified Retrieval across Heterogeneous Knowledge Sources

Published: 2026-05-27

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.29250

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper addresses unified retrieval across heterogeneous knowledge sources—including unstructured text, relational databases, RDF knowledge graphs, and property graphs—in the field of information retrieval and natural language processing.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Building on prior single-source retrievers and unified-representation methods that flatten structural differences into a shared embedding space, the paper proposes preserving each source's native query language and structural affordances through a unifying access layer rather than homogenization.
3. ❓ Problem: It solves the problem that existing retrieval systems are confined to a single knowledge source or lose expressive structural operators when heterogeneous sources are collapsed into a shared representation, fragmenting the broader knowledge landscape behind incompatible interfaces.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The authors present OmniRetrieval, a framework that employs a long-context LLM to select relevant sources from a catalog of heterogeneous descriptors, generates executable native queries for each source conditioned on its structural context, and consolidates heterogeneous executor outputs via cross-source evidence selection.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Evaluated on 13 datasets spanning 309 knowledge bases across four backend types, OmniRetrieval consistently outperforms single-backend and KB-routing baselines in source selection accuracy, retrieval accuracy, and LLM-as-a-Judge scores, demonstrating that coordinating native queries achieves effective unified retrieval.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 论文研究的是在信息检索与自然语言处理领域中，如何对非结构化文本、关系型数据库、RDF知识图谱和属性图等异构知识源进行统一检索。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 该研究基于现有的单源检索器和将异构知识源压缩为统一表示的相关工作，提出应通过统一访问层保留各知识源的原生查询语言与结构特性，而非对其进行同质化处理。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了现有检索方法只能处理单一知识源，或将异构源强行统一到共享表示时会丧失模式、本体和组合操作符等结构性能力，从而导致整体知识景观碎片化且难以访问的问题。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 作者提出了OmniRetrieval框架，利用长上下文大语言模型联合读取所有注册源的结构描述符以选择相关源，为每个源生成基于其结构上下文的本地可执行查询，并通过跨源证据选择步骤整合异构执行结果。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 在覆盖13个数据集和309个知识库的广泛基准上，OmniRetrieval在源选择准确率、检索准确率及LLM-as-a-Judge评分上均稳定优于单源基线和知识库路由基线，证明通过原生查询协调可实现高效统一检索。

minWM: A Full-Stack Open-Source Framework for Real-Time Interactive Video World Models

Published: 2026-05-27

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.30263

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents minWM, a full-stack open-source framework in the domain of real-time interactive video world models that converts bidirectional video diffusion foundation models into camera-controllable few-step autoregressive generators. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building upon existing bidirectional T2V/TI2V diffusion models and prior autoregressive distillation techniques like Causal Forcing, the paper proposes a unified, modular, and extensible end-to-end pipeline that integrates data construction, controllable fine-tuning, AR training, and few-step distillation into a single reproducible framework. 3. **❓ Problem:** It solves the problem that existing high-quality video diffusion models cannot be directly used as real-time interactive world models due to their lack of causal rollout, user controllability, and low-latency inference, while current solutions remain scattered across separate pipelines and lack reproducible full-stack implementations. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The method uses a two-phase pipeline: first fine-tuning bidirectional diffusion backbones with PRoPE for camera control, then applying Causal Forcing or Causal Forcing++ (comprising AR diffusion training, causal ODE or causal consistency distillation initialization, and asymmetric DMD) to distill the model into a few-step autoregressive generator. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** The framework achieves over 220× first-frame latency reduction on both Wan2.1-T2V-1.3B and HY1.5-TI2V-8B backbones while preserving camera controllability, supported by practical ablations on camera trajectory quality, training steps, and minimal batch-size requirements.
