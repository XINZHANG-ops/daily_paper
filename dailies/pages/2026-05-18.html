MMSkills: Towards Multimodal Skills for General Visual Agents

Published: 2026-05-13

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.13527

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper introduces MMSkills, a framework for equipping general visual agents with reusable multimodal procedural knowledge in the form of state-conditioned skill packages for GUI and game environments. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** It extends prior text- or code-based skill representations by formalizing multimodal procedural knowledge that couples textual procedures with runtime state cards and multi-view keyframes, and by introducing a branch-loaded consultation mechanism to use them. 3. **❓ Problem:** The paper solves the problem that existing skills are insufficient for visual agents because they lack visual grounding, addressing three practical challenges: representation (what the package contains), generation (how to build it from public trajectories), and utilization (how to consult it without context overload or over-anchoring). 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The method involves a multimodal skill package format (textual procedure, state cards, and multi-view keyframes), an automated trajectory-to-skill Generator that produces these packages from public non-evaluation trajectories, and a branch-loaded agent that isolates skill evidence in a temporary branch to distill structured guidance for the main agent. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Results show that MMSkills significantly improves task success rates and efficiency across GUI and game benchmarks for both frontier and smaller multimodal models compared to no-skill and text-only baselines, while ablations confirm the necessity of state cards, visual keyframes, and branch loading.

Causal Forcing++: Scalable Few-Step Autoregressive Diffusion Distillation for Real-Time Interactive Video Generation

Published: 2026-05-13

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.15141

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper focuses on real-time interactive video generation in the computer vision and generative AI domain, specifically proposing a scalable few-step autoregressive diffusion distillation method.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: It builds upon prior autoregressive diffusion distillation pipelines such as CausVid, Self Forcing, and Causal Forcing, and introduces causal consistency distillation (causal CD) as a principled, scalable substitute for causal ODE initialization to enable aggressive frame-wise 1–2 step generation.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper solves the initialization bottleneck for few-step autoregressive diffusion students, where existing strategies are either architecturally misaligned with causal rollout, incapable of few-step generation, or prohibitively expensive due to offline full PF-ODE trajectory generation and storage.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The proposed Causal Forcing++ pipeline uses a three-stage approach: teacher forcing AR diffusion training, causal consistency distillation that supervises the student via single online teacher ODE steps between adjacent timesteps instead of full offline trajectories, and asymmetric distribution matching distillation with self-rollout to distill a bidirectional model into a frame-wise few-step AR student.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Experiments on Wan2.1-1.3B demonstrate that the frame-wise 2-step model surpasses prior state-of-the-art chunk-wise 4-step methods in VBench Total, Quality, and VisionReward while reducing first-frame latency by 50% and Stage 2 training cost by a factor of 4.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 本文属于计算机视觉与生成式AI领域，研究用于实时交互式视频生成的可扩展少步自回归扩散蒸馏技术。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 该工作在CausVid、Self Forcing和Causal Forcing等现有自回归扩散蒸馏方法的基础上，提出将因果一致性蒸馏（causal CD）作为因果ODE初始化的理论等价且可扩展的替代方案，以实现帧级1-2步生成的激进低延迟目标。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了现有少步自回归学生模型初始化策略在帧级低延迟设定下因架构不对齐、缺乏少步生成能力或离线预计算完整PF-ODE轨迹成本过高而无法满足需求的瓶颈问题。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 方法采用三阶段流程：教师强制AR扩散训练、通过相邻时间步单步在线教师ODE监督的因果一致性蒸馏进行高效初始化、以及结合自滚动的非对称分布匹配蒸馏，从而将双向扩散模型蒸馏为帧级少步AR学生模型。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 基于Wan2.1-1.3B的实验在VBench和VisionReward基准上表明，帧级2步的Causal Forcing++超越了现有最先进的块级4步方法，首帧延迟降低50%，第二阶段训练成本减少为1/4。
