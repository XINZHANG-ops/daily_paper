OpenWorldLib: A Unified Codebase and Definition of Advanced World Models

Published: 2026-04-05

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.04707

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper addresses the field of Advanced World Models in artificial intelligence, focusing on creating a unified framework for perception, interaction, reasoning, and memory capabilities needed to understand and predict the physical world.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: The paper builds upon the evolution of world models (from Ha & Schmidhuber's initial work to recent VLMs and diffusion-based approaches) and introduces a standardized definition: a world model is centered on perception with interaction and long-term memory capabilities, and proposes OpenWorldLib as a unified codebase integrating diverse world model tasks.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper solves the problem of lacking a clear, unified definition and standardized framework for world models, where existing tasks like interactive video generation, multimodal reasoning, 3D generation, and VLA are fragmented and inconsistently defined.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The paper designs OpenWorldLib with five core modules: Operator (input standardization), Synthesis (visual/audio/action generation), Reasoning (spatial/omnireasoning), Representation (3D reconstruction), and Memory (long-term contextual storage), coordinated by a unified Pipeline module.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: The framework was evaluated on interactive video generation (Matrix-Game-2, Lingbot-World, Hunyuan-GameCraft, Cosmos), 3D scene generation (VGGT, FlashWorld), multimodal reasoning, and VLA tasks (AI2-THOR, LIBERO), demonstrating capabilities across navigation, scene reconstruction, and embodied agent control.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 论文聚焦于人工智能中的高级世界模型领域，致力于构建一个统一框架，以实现对物理世界的感知、交互、推理和记忆能力。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 论文基于世界模型的演进历程（从Ha和Schmidhuber的早期工作到近期的VLM和扩散模型方法），提出标准化定义：世界模型是以感知为中心、具备交互和长期记忆能力的模型，并创建OpenWorldLib作为整合各类世界模型任务的统一代码库。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了世界模型缺乏清晰统一定义和标准化框架的问题，当前如交互式视频生成、多模态推理、3D生成和VLA等任务分散且定义不一致。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 论文设计了OpenWorldLib框架，包含五个核心模块：Operator（输入标准化）、Synthesis（视觉/音频/动作生成）、Reasoning（空间/全模态推理）、Representation（3D重建）和Memory（长期上下文存储），由统一的Pipeline模块协调。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 框架在交互式视频生成（Matrix-Game-2、灵Bot-World、Hunyuan-GameCraft、 Cosmos）、3D场景生成（VGGT、FlashWorld）、多模态推理和VLA任务（AI2-THOR、LIBERO）上进行评估，展示了导航、场景重建和具身智能体控制等能力。

MinerU2.5-Pro: Pushing the Limits of Data-Centric Document Parsing at Scale

Published: 2026-04-05

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.04771

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: Document parsing, which converts unstructured PDFs into structured machine-readable formats (e.g., Markdown), with a focus on data-centric AI approaches rather than architectural innovation.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Based on MinerU2.5's 1.2B-parameter architecture; new idea that performance bottlenecks stem from shared training data deficiencies (insufficient coverage, unreliable annotations on hard samples) rather than model architecture itself.
3. ❓ Problem: Limited training data coverage (less than 10M samples with long-tail distribution) and annotation quality paradox where hard samples that matter most are least reliably annotatable.
4. 🛠️ Methods: A Data Engine co-designed around coverage, informativeness, and accuracy with three components: Diversity-and-Difficulty-Aware Sampling (DDAS), Cross-Model Consistency Verification (CMCV), and Judge-and-Refine annotation pipeline; plus a three-stage progressive training strategy.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Achieves 95.69 on OmniDocBench v1.6 (Full), improving 2.71 points over the MinerU2.5 baseline (+2.43 on Hard subset), surpassing all existing methods without any architectural modification.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 文档解析，即将非结构化的PDF等文档转换为结构化的机器可读格式（如Markdown），重点关注数据-centric AI方法而非模型架构创新。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 基于MinerU2.5的1.2B参数架构；新思路是性能瓶颈源于训练数据的共同缺陷（覆盖不足、对困难样本的标注不可靠），而非模型架构本身。
3. ❓ 问题: 训练数据覆盖有限（不足10M样本，呈长尾分布），且标注质量存在悖论——对模型提升最重要的困难样本反而最难可靠标注。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 数据引擎围绕覆盖度、信息量和标注准确性协同设计，包含三个组件：多样性-难度感知采样（DDAS）、跨模型一致性验证（CMCV）和判断-修正标注流水线；另采用三阶段渐进式训练策略。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 在OmniDocBench v1.6（Full）上达到95.69分，较MinerU2.5基线提升2.71分（Hard子集提升2.43分），在未修改任何架构的情况下超越所有现有方法。

TriAttention: Efficient Long Reasoning with Trigonometric KV Compression

Published: 2026-04-05

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.04921

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper addresses KV cache compression for efficient long-context reasoning in large language models. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Existing methods estimate token importance from post‑RoPE attention, while this work introduces pre‑RoPE Q/K concentration and a trigonometric series for importance scoring. 3. **❓ Problem:** Long chain‑of‑thought reasoning generates massive KV caches that cause memory bottlenecks, and prior post‑RoPE based compression methods are unstable due to limited observation windows. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** TriAttention scores keys using a trigonometric series derived from Q/K centers plus norm‑based signals, adaptively weighted by concentration, then prunes the KV cache to retain top‑scoring entries. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** On AIME25 and MATH 500, TriAttention matches Full Attention accuracy while delivering 2.5× throughput gain or 10.7× KV memory reduction, outperforming SnapKV and R‑KV baselines.
