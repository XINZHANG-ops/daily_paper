Stream-T1: Test-Time Scaling for Streaming Video Generation

Published: 2026-05-05

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.04461

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper proposes Stream-T1, a Test-Time Scaling framework tailored for streaming video generation, falling within the domain of computer vision and generative AI. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building upon existing Test-Time Scaling methods and streaming video generation models, the paper introduces the novel idea of applying TTS to chunk-level streaming generation with active optimization of latent noise and memory rather than passive candidate selection. 3. **❓ Problem:** It solves the problem of prohibitive computational costs and lack of fine-grained temporal guidance in existing video TTS methods, which globally denoise entire videos instead of leveraging the intrinsic efficiency of streaming chunk synthesis. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The method employs three units: Stream-Scaled Noise Propagation to refine initial noise using historical trajectories, Stream-Scaled Reward Pruning to balance local spatial aesthetics and global temporal coherence via a dynamic long-short reward, and Stream-Scaled Memory Sinking to dynamically route evicted KV-cache into discard, EMA, or append pathways based on semantic boundary detection. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Extensive experiments on 5-second and 30-second video benchmarks demonstrate state-of-the-art performance, significantly surpassing baselines in temporal consistency, motion smoothness, and visual quality.

Stream-R1: Reliability-Perplexity Aware Reward Distillation for Streaming Video Generation

Published: 2026-05-04

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.03849

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper studies streaming video generation in computer vision, specifically distillation-based acceleration for autoregressive video diffusion models.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: It extends distribution matching distillation (DMD) by newly identifying two axes of variance in its supervision signal: Inter-Reliability across rollouts and Intra-Perplexity across spatiotemporal elements within each rollout.
3. ❓ Problem: Existing DMD methods apply uniform weighting to every rollout, frame, and pixel, ignoring that supervision reliability varies across rollouts and that different spatiotemporal regions offer unequal potential for refinement, which caps distilled quality.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The authors propose Stream-R1, which uses a single pretrained video reward model to exponentially reweight rollout-level loss by reward scores (Inter-Reliability) and to factorize per-pixel reward-gradient saliency into spatial and temporal weights (Intra-Perplexity), with adaptive balancing across visual quality, motion quality, and text alignment.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Stream-R1 achieves the highest VBench total and quality scores among all compared methods, surpassing even its multi-step teacher Wan2.1, while demonstrating superior long-video temporal consistency and winning both VLM-based and human preference evaluations without architectural changes or extra inference cost.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 论文主题为计算机视觉中的流式视频生成，聚焦于自回归视频扩散模型的蒸馏加速。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 该研究在分布匹配蒸馏（DMD）基础上，新发现了监督信号中跨生成样本的可靠性差异（Inter-Reliability）与样本内时空区域的优化潜力差异（Intra-Perplexity）这两个关键维度。
3. ❓ 问题: 现有方法对所有生成样本、帧和像素赋予统一的蒸馏权重，忽视了监督信号在不同样本上的可靠性差异以及单个样本内各时空区域改进潜力不均的问题，导致蒸馏质量存在上界瓶颈。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 作者提出Stream-R1框架，利用单一预训练视频奖励模型，通过指数奖励重加权实现样本级可靠性感知（Inter-Reliability），并通过奖励梯度反传提取像素级显著性、分解为空间与时间权重实现元素级困惑度感知（Intra-Perplexity），辅以视觉质量、运动质量和文本对齐的多维度自适应平衡。
5. 📊 结果与评估: Stream-R1在标准基准上取得最高VBench总分与质量分，甚至超越其多步教师模型Wan2.1，在长视频生成中表现出更优的时间一致性与更低漂移，并在VLM与人工偏好评估中全面领先，且无需修改学生架构或增加推理开销。
