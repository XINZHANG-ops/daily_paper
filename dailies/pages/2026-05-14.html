MinT: Managed Infrastructure for Training and Serving Millions of LLMs

Published: 2026-05-12

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.13779

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper introduces MinT, a managed infrastructure system in the machine learning systems domain for Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) post-training and online serving of millions of Large Language Model policies over shared resident base models. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building on LoRA, Tinker, and multi-LoRA serving systems, the paper proposes treating compact exported LoRA adapter revisions—not full checkpoints—as the primary managed unit moving between training and serving, while separating durable policy addressability from bounded engine-local execution. 3. **❓ Problem:** Traditional LLM post-training infrastructure requires materializing and moving full model checkpoints for every policy variant, which is prohibitively expensive and unscalable for managing large, continuously evolving populations of trained behaviors over shared frontier-scale base models. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** MinT keeps base models resident and moves only LoRA adapter revisions through the lifecycle, scaling via model-parallel training and serving beyond 1T parameters, adapter-only handoffs under 1% of base size, and tiered caching with packed MoE tensors to support million-scale policy catalogs. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Evaluation demonstrates adapter-only handoff reduces latency by up to 18.3× on dense models and 2.85× on MoE models, concurrent multi-policy GRPO shortens wall time by 1.45–1.77× without extra memory, and the serving plane supports 10^6 addressable policies with 8.5–8.7× faster live engine loading via packed tensors, validated on models exceeding 1T parameters.

Published: 2026-05-12

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.13724

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents AnyFlow, a novel any-step video diffusion distillation framework in the domain of generative AI and computer vision. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building upon consistency distillation and flow map theory, the paper introduces the first flow-map-based video distillation framework that learns transitions between arbitrary time pairs instead of fixed endpoint mappings. 3. **❓ Problem:** It addresses the degradation issue in consistency-distilled video models where performance declines as more sampling steps are allocated at test time due to trajectory drift away from the PF-ODE path. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The method employs a two-stage pipeline comprising improved forward flow map training for initialization and on-policy flow map distillation with Flow Map Backward Simulation to optimize full ODE trajectories via shortcut transitions. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Extensive experiments demonstrate that AnyFlow matches or surpasses consistency-based counterparts in few-step generation while scaling effectively with increased steps, achieving state-of-the-art results across causal and bidirectional architectures from 1.3B to 14B parameters.

Training Long-Context Vision-Language Models Effectively with Generalization Beyond 128K Context

Published: 2026-05-12

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.13831

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents a systematic study of long-context continued pre-training (LongPT) for large vision-language models (LVLMs) to effectively extend their context windows beyond 128K tokens.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Building upon LLM context-extension methods and existing LVLMs, the paper introduces the insight that instruction-formatted long-document VQA with balanced length distributions and retrieval-heavy mixtures provides superior supervision for LongPT compared to OCR transcription.
3. ❓ Problem: It solves the problem of lacking practical, data-efficient training recipes for developing long-context vision-language models, specifically regarding how to curate and balance multimodal long-context data mixtures without degrading short-context performance.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The method involves synthesizing long-document VQA data through a segment-level pipeline, conducting extensive ablations on length distributions, extraction-to-reasoning ratios, and short-data mixing, and training MMProLong via continued pre-training with Dynamic-NTK RoPE scaling.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: MMProLong achieves a 7.1% improvement in long-document VQA, generalizes robustly to 256K and 512K contexts without additional training, and transfers to webpage needle retrieval, vision-text compression, and long-video understanding benchmarks.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 本文系统研究了大型视觉语言模型（LVLM）的长上下文持续预训练（LongPT），以将其上下文窗口有效扩展至128K以上。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 基于大语言模型上下文扩展技术与现有视觉语言模型，本文提出指令格式的长文档视觉问答配合平衡的长度分布和偏重检索的数据混合，是比OCR转录更有效的LongPT监督方式。
3. ❓ 问题: 本文解决了长上下文视觉语言模型实用训练方案不足的问题，特别是如何设计和平衡多模态长上下文数据混合以扩展上下文窗口，同时避免短上下文能力退化。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 论文采用段落级合成管道构建长文档VQA数据，系统消融了序列长度分布、提取与推理任务比例及短数据混合比例，并结合Dynamic-NTK RoPE缩放训练得到MMProLong。
5. 📊 结果与评估: MMProLong在长文档VQA上提升7.1%，无需额外训练即可泛化至256K和512K上下文，并在网页多模态大海捞针、视觉-文本压缩和长视频理解等任务上展现出良好的迁移能力。
