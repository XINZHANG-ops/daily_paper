Learnable Multipliers: Freeing the Scale of Language Model Matrix Layers

Published: 2026-01-08

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.04890

1. üìò Topic and Domain: The paper explores learnable multipliers in language model matrix layers, focusing on improving neural network optimization and training dynamics in large language models.
2. üí° Previous Research and New Ideas: Based on research showing weight decay and stochastic gradient noise create an equilibrium in matrix weights, proposes new learnable multipliers to free matrix layers from this constraining equilibrium.
3. ‚ùì Problem: Matrix layers in neural networks are trapped in a suboptimal noise-weight decay equilibrium that prevents them from learning optimal scales based on training data.
4. üõ†Ô∏è Methods: Introduces learnable scalar and vector multipliers to matrix layers, allowing them to adapt their scale freely while maintaining stability through careful placement and light weight decay.
5. üìä Results and Evaluation: The learnable multipliers improved model performance across various benchmarks, with ~1.2% average improvement in downstream tasks for both Adam and Muon optimizers, while requiring no additional inference compute.
