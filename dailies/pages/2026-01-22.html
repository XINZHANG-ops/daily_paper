RubricHub: A Comprehensive and Highly Discriminative Rubric Dataset via Automated Coarse-to-Fine Generation

Published: 2026-01-13

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.08430

1. üìò Topic and Domain: The paper focuses on automated rubric generation for evaluating open-ended LLM outputs across multiple domains including medical, science, writing, instruction-following, and chat.
2. üí° Previous Research and New Ideas: Building on existing rubric-based evaluation methods and LLM-as-judge paradigms, the paper proposes a novel Coarse-to-Fine Rubric Generation framework that synergizes principle-guided synthesis, multi-model aggregation, and difficulty evolution to create highly discriminative evaluation criteria.
3. ‚ùì Problem: The paper addresses the challenge of evaluating open-ended LLM outputs that lack ground truth, where existing rubric-based methods suffer from manual creation bottlenecks, narrow domain coverage, and low discriminability leading to supervision ceiling effects.
4. üõ†Ô∏è Methods: The authors employ a three-stage automated framework: response-grounded and principle-guided generation, multi-model aggregation to reduce bias, and difficulty evolution to create fine-grained criteria, followed by two-stage post-training using Rubric-based Rejection Sampling Fine-Tuning (RuFT) and Rubric-based Reinforcement Learning (RuRL).
5. üìä Results and Evaluation: The resulting RubricHub dataset (~110k rubrics) enables Qwen3-14B to achieve state-of-the-art performance on HealthBench (69.3), surpassing GPT-5 (67.2), with consistent improvements across five evaluation domains demonstrating the framework's effectiveness in providing dense supervision signals for LLM alignment.

ACoT-VLA: Action Chain-of-Thought for Vision-Language-Action Models

Published: 2026-01-16

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.11404

1. üìò Topic and Domain: The paper focuses on Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for robotic manipulation, specifically introducing Action Chain-of-Thought reasoning for generalist robot policies.
2. üí° Previous Research and New Ideas: The paper builds on existing VLA models that use VLM embeddings and intermediate reasoning (language sub-tasks or visual goal images), but proposes a novel approach where reasoning occurs directly in the action space through explicit action trajectories and implicit action priors.
3. ‚ùì Problem: The paper addresses the semantic-kinematic gap between high-level multimodal inputs and low-level action execution, where existing approaches rely on indirect language or vision guidance that fails to convey granular action-space information needed for precise robotic control.
4. üõ†Ô∏è Methods: The authors propose ACoT-VLA with two components: an Explicit Action Reasoner (EAR) that generates coarse reference trajectories using a transformer architecture, and an Implicit Action Reasoner (IAR) that extracts latent action priors from VLM representations through cross-attention mechanisms.
5. üìä Results and Evaluation: The method achieves state-of-the-art performance with 98.5% success rate on LIBERO, 84.1% on LIBERO-Plus, and 47.4% on VLABench benchmarks, demonstrating significant improvements especially in long-horizon tasks and under distribution shifts.
