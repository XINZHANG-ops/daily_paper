SkillClaw: Let Skills Evolve Collectively with Agentic Evolver

Published: 2026-04-08

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.08377

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper addresses collective skill evolution for LLM agents in multi-user ecosystems, belonging to the domain of autonomous AI agents and machine learning systems.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: The paper builds upon existing agent self-evolution research (e.g., Reflexion, memory-based methods) and skill-based approaches (e.g., skill libraries, transferable skills), introducing the novel concept of collective skill evolution where knowledge from individual user interactions contributes to a shared, continuously improving skill ecosystem through an autonomous agentic evolver.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper solves the problem of static skill ecosystems in LLM agents—skills remain unchanged after deployment, causing repeated rediscovery of solutions across users and preventing system-level knowledge accumulation.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The paper proposes SkillClaw, a framework using a centralized evolution architecture with an agentic evolver that aggregates interaction trajectories across users, groups them by referenced skills, and performs evidence-driven skill updates (refinement or creation) with nighttime validation before synchronization.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Experiments on WildClawBench show substantial improvements, including +52% relative gain in Search & Retrieval, +88% in Creative Synthesis, +33% in Safety & Alignment, and +12% in Social Interaction, demonstrating effective multi-user driven collective evolution.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 该论文聚焦于多用户生态系统中的LLM智能体集体技能演化，属于自主AI智能体与机器学习系统领域。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 该论文基于现有智能体自我演化研究（如Reflexion、记忆方法）和技能方法（如技能库、可迁移技能），提出集体技能演化这一新概念——通过自主智能体进化器，使个体用户交互产生的知识贡献于共享且持续改进的技能生态系统。
3. ❓ 问题: 该论文解决的是LLM智能体中技能静态化的核心问题——技能在部署后保持不变，导致跨用户的解决方案重复发现，无法实现系统级知识积累。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 论文提出SkillClaw框架，采用集中式演化架构，配备智能体进化器，跨用户聚合交互轨迹，按引用技能分组，在夜间验证后执行基于证据的技能更新（改进或创建），再同步至所有智能体。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 在WildClawBench上的实验显示显著提升：搜索与检索提升52%、创意合成提升88%、安全与对齐提升33%、社交交互提升12%，证明了多用户驱动集体演化的有效性。

When Numbers Speak: Aligning Textual Numerals and Visual Instances in Text-to-Video Diffusion Models

Published: 2026-04-08

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.08546

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper focuses on aligning textual numerals with visual object instances in text-to-video diffusion models, belonging to the domain of generative AI for video synthesis. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building on Diffusion Transformer (DiT) video generation and attention-based editing, the authors propose a training-free identify‑then‑guide paradigm that leverages discriminative self‑/cross‑attention heads to construct a countable layout and guide regeneration. 3. **❓ Problem:** Existing T2V models struggle to accurately generate the number of objects specified in prompts due to weak numerical grounding and limited instance separability in the latent space. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** NUMINA uses a two‑phase pipeline: first it selects the most instance‑separable attention heads to build a countable latent layout, then it refines the layout (adding/removing objects) and modulates cross‑attention via bias adjustments to steer regeneration without additional training. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** NUMINA improves counting accuracy by up to 7.4% on Wan2.1‑1.3B, 4.9% on the 5B model, and 5.5% on the 14B model, while maintaining or enhancing temporal consistency and CLIP alignment, demonstrating its effectiveness across multiple scales and compatibility with other techniques.

MegaStyle: Constructing Diverse and Scalable Style Dataset via Consistent Text-to-Image Style Mapping

Published: 2026-04-08

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.08364

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper focuses on constructing a large-scale, high-quality style dataset for image style transfer using consistent text-to-image mapping from large generative models.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Based on existing style datasets (WikiArt, JourneyDB, OmniStyle-150K) and style transfer methods, the paper proposes using the consistent T2I style mapping capability of modern generative models like Qwen-Image to generate intra-style consistent, inter-style diverse style pairs.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper solves the problem of lacking intra-style consistency, inter-style diversity, and high-quality in existing style datasets, which hinder reliable style similarity measurement and generalizable style transfer.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The paper uses a three-stage data curation pipeline (Image Pool Collection, Prompt Curation and Balance, Style Image Generation), style-supervised contrastive learning (SSCL) to train MegaStyle-Encoder, and paired supervision to train MegaStyle-FLUX on FLUX.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: MegaStyle-1.4M achieves high intra-style consistency with 8,355 overall artistic styles; MegaStyle-Encoder outperforms CLIP, CSD, and SigLIP with mAP@1 of 88.46%; MegaStyle-FLUX achieves highest text alignment (23.20) and second-best style alignment (76.16) with best human preference scores.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 该论文专注于利用大规模生成模型的文本到图像一致映射能力，构建用于图像风格迁移的大规模、高质量风格数据集。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 基于现有风格数据集（WikiArt、JourneyDB、OmniStyle-150K）和风格迁移方法，论文提出利用现代生成模型（如Qwen-Image）的一致文本到图像风格映射能力来生成同风格一致、跨风格多样的风格图像对。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了现有风格数据集缺乏同风格一致性、跨风格多样性和高质量的问题，这些问题阻碍了可靠的风格相似度测量和可泛化的风格迁移。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 论文采用三阶段数据策展流程（图像池收集、提示策展与平衡、风格图像生成），使用风格监督对比学习（SSCL）训练MegaStyle-Encoder，并使用配对监督在FLUX上训练MegaStyle-FLUX。
5. 📊 结果与评估: MegaStyle-1.4M拥有8,355种整体艺术风格，实现了高同风格一致性；MegaStyle-Encoder以88.46%的mAP@1得分超越CLIP、CSD和SigLIP；MegaStyle-FLUX达到最高文本对齐度（23.20）和第二高风格对齐度（76.16），人类偏好评分最佳。 ===

Today's Reading Tips 今日阅读推荐

Start with the SkillClaw paper, as it introduces a novel collective skill evolution framework for LLM agents—a fundamental advance that underpins many downstream AI tasks. After that, explore NUMINA to see how counting can be enforced in video generation, and then MegaStyle to learn how large‑scale style datasets can improve controllable image style transfer.
建议首先阅读 SkillClaw 论文，因为它提出了一种新颖的集体技能进化框架，能够提升 LLM 智能体的学习和适应能力，是后续研究的重要基础。随后可阅读 NUMINA，了解如何在视频生成中实现精准的物体计数，最后再阅读 MegaStyle，学习如何构建大规模、风格一致的数据集，以推动风格迁移的可控生成。
