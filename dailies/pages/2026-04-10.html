Published: 2026-04-08

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.08523

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper focuses on evaluating AI agents' ability to perform everyday online tasks (e.g., booking flights, shopping, job applications) on live production websites. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** The work builds on prior web-agent benchmarks (e.g., WebArena, OSWorld) but introduces a real-world live-website evaluation framework with safe request interception and a five-layer behavioral recording system. 3. **❓ Problem:** Existing benchmarks evaluate agents in static sandboxes or read-only tasks, lacking coverage of write-heavy, state-changing web interactions that are common in daily life. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The authors develop a Chrome extension with CDP instrumentation to intercept final submission requests, record five layers of behavior (session replay, screenshots, HTTP traffic, agent messages, browser actions), and use an agentic evaluator to compare agent trajectories against human ground truth. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Experiments on 7 frontier models show that even the best model (Claude Sonnet 4.6) achieves only 33.3% success rate on ClawBench, revealing a large gap between benchmark performance and real-world everyday web task competence.

HY-Embodied-0.5: Embodied Foundation Models for Real-World Agents

Published: 2026-04-07

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.07430

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents HY-Embodied-0.5, a family of embodied foundation models designed for real-world agents, targeting the domain of embodied AI, vision-language models, and robotic control.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Based on Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and Mixture-of-Transformers (MoT) architectures; introduces novel visual latent tokens, iterative self-evolving post-training with RL and rejection sampling fine-tuning, and on-policy distillation from large to small models.
3. ❓ Problem: The paper addresses the gap between general VLMs and embodied agent requirements, specifically fine-grained visual perception for physical grounding and embodied prediction, interaction, and planning capabilities for real-world deployment.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The methods include a Mixture-of-Transformers architecture enabling modality-adaptive computing, HY-ViT 2.0 native-resolution visual encoder, large-scale pre-training (600B+ tokens), embodied-spatial mid-training, task-aware reinforcement learning with hybrid rewards, iterative self-evolution, and large-to-small on-policy distillation.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: MoT-2B achieves state-of-the-art on 16/22 benchmarks, outperforming Qwen3-VL-4B by 10.2% and RoboBrain2.5-4B by 8.6%; MoE-A32B achieves 67.0% average, surpassing Gemini 3.0 Pro (63.6%); robot experiments show 85%, 80%, and 75% success rates on packing, stacking, and hanging tasks.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 本论文提出了 HY-Embodied-0.5，一个为真实世界智能体设计的具身基础模型家族，领域涉及具身人工智能、视觉-语言模型和机器人控制。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 基于视觉-语言模型（VLM）和混合Transformer（MoT）架构；创新性地引入视觉潜令牌、结合强化学习与拒绝采样微调的迭代自我进化后训练范式，以及从大模型到小模型的在策略蒸馏。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决通用视觉-语言模型与具身智能体需求之间的差距，包括物理世界精粒度视觉感知、具身预测、交互与规划能力，以及边缘部署的高效模型需求。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 采用支持模态自适应计算的混合Transformer架构、HY-ViT 2.0原生分辨率视觉编码器、600B+token大规模预训练、具身-空间中期训练、任务感知强化学习（混合奖励）、迭代自我进化、以及大到小模型的在策略蒸馏。
5. 📊 结果与评估: MoT-2B在22个基准中的16个上达到最优，相比Qwen3-VL-4B提升10.2%、相比RoboBrain2.5-4B提升8.6%；MoE-A32B达到67.0%平均分，超越Gemini 3.0 Pro（63.6%）；机器人实验在包装、堆叠、悬挂任务上分别达到85%、80%、75%的成功率。
