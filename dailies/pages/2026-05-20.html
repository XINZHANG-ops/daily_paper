Paper 1

GoLongRL: Capability-Oriented Long Context Reinforcement Learning with Multitask Alignment

Published: 2026-05-18

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.19577

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents a fully open-source, capability-oriented post-training framework for long-context reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR) in the domain of large language models. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** It builds upon existing long-context RL methods such as GRPO and QwenLong-L1.5, and introduces a capability-oriented dataset spanning 9 diverse task types with heterogeneous natural rewards alongside a novel TMN-Reweight algorithm for multitask optimization. 3. **❓ Problem:** It solves the limitations of existing approaches that suffer from homogeneous retrieval-centric task coverage, narrow reward design, and optimization instability caused by per-prompt normalization and cross-task reward scale inconsistency in heterogeneous multitask settings. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The authors construct a 23K-sample dataset across 9 capability-oriented tasks paired with their natural evaluation metrics and propose TMN-Reweight, which combines task-level mean normalization for cross-task reward scale alignment with difficulty-adaptive four-quadrant reweighting for reliable advantage estimation. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Experiments show that vanilla GRPO trained on their dataset significantly outperforms QwenLong-L1.5, and TMN-Reweight further improves average performance while preserving or enhancing general reasoning, agentic memory, dialogue memory, and extrapolation to sequences up to 1M tokens.

OpenComputer: Verifiable Software Worlds for Computer-Use Agents

Published: 2026-05-18

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.19769

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper introduces OpenComputer, an AI and human-computer interaction framework for synthesizing verifiable software worlds to benchmark and train computer-use agents across real desktop applications. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** It departs from prior manually curated benchmarks and LLM-as-judge evaluation by making hard-coded, self-evolving programmatic verifiers the organizing principle for automatic task synthesis and reliable state inspection. 3. **❓ Problem:** It solves the dual bottlenecks of scalably building realistic, reproducible desktop environments and trustworthily verifying fine-grained application state that visual proxies or LLM judges often fail to catch. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The framework combines app-specific state verifiers with structured inspection endpoints, a self-evolving verification layer refined by execution-grounded feedback, a verifier-aware pipeline that synthesizes machine-checkable tasks, and an evaluation harness that computes auditable partial-credit rewards. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Experiments show frontier agents achieve at most 68.3% task success, hard-coded verifiers align better with human adjudication than LLM judges (97.3% vs 92.2%), and the self-evolving layer repairs 89.4% of checker-side errors while improving human-checker agreement to 94.1%.

Today's Reading Tips 今日阅读推荐

I recommend starting with the OpenComputer paper, as its focus on hard-coded, self-evolving programmatic verifiers establishes a foundational paradigm for trustworthy agent evaluation that complements the verifiable reward designs in the long-context RL work and the deterministic verification gates in AutoResearchClaw. Together, these three papers advance a unified theme: replacing LLM-as-judge with objective, automated verification across training, benchmarking, and autonomous research pipelines.
建议优先阅读 OpenComputer 论文，它对硬编码、自进化程序验证器的关注为可信智能体评估奠定了范式基础，与长上下文 RL 中的可验证奖励设计以及 AutoResearchClaw 中的确定性验证门直接互补。这三篇论文共同推进了一个统一主题：在训练、基准测试和自主研究流程中以客观自动化验证取代 LLM-as-judge。
