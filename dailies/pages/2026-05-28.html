Paper 1

ProRL: Effective Reinforcement Learning for Proactive Recommendation via Rectified Policy Gradient Estimation

Published: 2026-05-26

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.28293

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents ProRL, a reinforcement learning framework for proactive recommendation systems that optimizes sequential guidance paths through rectified policy gradient estimation. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** It builds upon existing supervised, heuristic, and LLM-based proactive recommendation approaches and introduces Stepwise Reward Centering and Position-Specific Advantage Estimation as novel rectifications to standard policy gradients. 3. **❓ Problem:** It addresses the failure of standard policy gradient methods in proactive recommendation due to the length shortcut bias and high gradient variance inherent in path-level reward optimization. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** ProRL employs Stepwise Reward Centering to neutralize length-dependent bias and Position-Specific Advantage Estimation to reduce variance, implementing these within a pretrained T5-based sequence-to-sequence model fine-tuned via policy gradients on semantic item IDs. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Experiments on MovieLens-1M, Steam, and Amazon-Book datasets demonstrate that ProRL significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods in guidance effectiveness and path feasibility, validated by ablations and cross-evaluator analysis.

Paper 3

Gamma-World: Generative Multi-Agent World Modeling Beyond Two Players

Published: 2026-05-26

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.28816

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper introduces Gamma-World, a generative multi-agent world model for interactive simulation in gaming and robotics, situated in computer vision and video generation.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Extending beyond prior single-agent world models and the two-player Solaris system, the paper proposes Simplex Rotary Agent Encoding and Sparse Hub Attention to achieve scalable, permutation-symmetric multi-agent interaction.
3. ❓ Problem: It solves the problem of building controllable multi-agent world models that maintain temporal and cross-view consistency without quadratic attention costs or fixed slot identities.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The method uses simplex-based rotary embeddings for symmetric agent identity, learnable hub tokens for linear-cost cross-agent attention, and teacher-student distillation with KV caching for real-time causal rollout.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Gamma-World outperforms baselines in fidelity and consistency on multiplayer Minecraft, generalizes from two to four players without retraining, and achieves 24 FPS streaming with linearly scaling computation.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 论文提出 Gamma-World，一个面向游戏与机器人交互式模拟的生成式多智能体世界模型，属于计算机视觉与视频生成领域。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 在单智能体世界模型和双玩家系统 Solaris 的基础上，论文提出单纯形旋转智能体编码与稀疏中心注意力，以实现可扩展且排列对称的多智能体交互。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了构建可控多智能体世界模型的难题，使其在保持时序与跨视角一致性的同时，避免二次方注意力开销和固定槽位身份的限制。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 方法采用基于单纯形的旋转嵌入实现对称智能体身份，利用可学习中心令牌实现线性复杂度跨智能体注意力，并通过教师-学生蒸馏与 KV 缓存实现实时因果生成。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 结果表明，Gamma-World 在多人 Minecraft 上的保真度与一致性优于基线，无需重训练即可从两人泛化至四人，并以线性增长的计算成本实现 24 FPS 流式推理。

Today's Reading Tips 今日阅读推荐

I recommend starting with the ProRL paper, as its policy gradient rectifications for sequential path optimization offer a foundational perspective on the RL training dynamics that AXPO later addresses in multimodal agentic reasoning. Both papers diagnose and fix critical policy gradient pathologies—length bias and variance in ProRL versus sparse tool-use signals in AXPO—making them strongly connected reads, while Gamma-World complements them with a generative multi-agent world modeling approach.
建议优先阅读 ProRL 论文，其针对序列路径优化的策略梯度修正为理解 AXPO 在多模态智能体推理中处理的强化学习训练动态奠定了基础。这两篇论文分别诊断并修复了策略梯度的关键病理问题——ProRL 中的长度偏差与方差、AXPO 中稀疏的工具调用信号——因此是紧密相关的阅读；而 Gamma-World 则从生成式多智能体世界建模的角度提供了补充。
