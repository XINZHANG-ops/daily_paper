IndusAgent: Reinforcing Open-Vocabulary Industrial Anomaly Detection with Agentic Tools

Published: 2026-05-19

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.20682

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents an open-vocabulary industrial anomaly detection framework that augments multimodal large language models with autonomous agentic tools. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** It builds on multimodal LLMs and tool-augmented agents, introducing an active inspector paradigm with a tool-integrated reasoning corpus and accuracy-gated reinforcement learning. 3. **❓ Problem:** It addresses domain-misaligned reasoning, structural hallucinations, perceptual dilution, and poor open-vocabulary generalization in standard MLLMs for industrial inspection. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The method employs a three-stage pipeline: constructing the Indus-CoT dataset for supervised fine-tuning, then applying tool-augmented GRPO with an accuracy-gated reward to dynamically orchestrate external tools. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** IndusAgent achieves state-of-the-art zero-shot performance across five benchmarks with an average accuracy of 83.4%, significantly outperforming commercial and open-source baselines.

You Only Need Minimal RLVR Training: Extrapolating LLMs via Rank-1 Trajectories

Published: 2026-05-19

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.21468

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper investigates the low-rank geometric structure of RLVR weight trajectories in large language models and proposes a compute-efficient checkpoint extrapolation method. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building on prior findings that RLVR updates are structured and low-dimensional, the authors newly demonstrate that rank-1 parameter deltas per tensor capture the majority of downstream performance gains and evolve near-linearly with training steps. 3. **❓ Problem:** It addresses the prohibitive computational cost of full RLVR training by enabling accurate prediction of future high-performing checkpoints from only early training dynamics. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The authors introduce RELEX, a training-free algorithm that performs per-tensor SVD on early checkpoint trajectories to identify a rank-1 subspace and extrapolates future weights via linear regression of the projected scalar coefficients. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Across three Qwen models, RELEX matches or exceeds full RLVR accuracy on in-domain and out-of-domain math benchmarks with merely 15–20% of training steps, and can stably extrapolate to checkpoints 10–20× beyond the observation window.

Paper 3

Mega-ASR: Towards In-the-wild^2 Speech Recognition via Scaling up Real-world Acoustic Simulation

Published: 2026-05-18

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.19833

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper proposes MEGA-ASR, a unified framework for robust automatic speech recognition in complex real-world acoustic environments, situated in the speech processing and machine learning domain.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Building on existing ASR foundation models and large audio-language models, the paper introduces scalable compound acoustic simulation and progressive acoustic-to-semantic optimization to tackle severe, compositional in-the-wild distortions.
3. ❓ Problem: It addresses the "acoustic robustness bottleneck" where current models suffer severe hallucinations and omissions under real-world conditions featuring simultaneous, physically plausible compound acoustic degradations.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The method comprises the large-scale VOICES-IN-THE-WILD-2M dataset with 54 compound scenarios, Acoustic-to-Semantic Progressive Supervised Fine-Tuning for staged capability building, and Dual-Granularity WER-Gated Policy Optimization with dynamic token-level and sentence-level rewards.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: MEGA-ASR achieves state-of-the-art robustness on adverse benchmarks (e.g., 45.69% vs. 54.01% WER on VOiCES R4-B-F), delivers over 30% relative WER reduction on compositional scenarios, and preserves competitive clean-speech performance via environment-aware routing.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 论文提出MEGA-ASR框架，致力于通过规模化真实世界声学模拟实现野外鲁棒语音识别，属于语音处理与人工智能领域。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 该工作基于ASR基础模型与大型音频语言模型，创新性地提出可扩展的复合声学数据构建与渐进式声学到语义优化范式。
3. ❓ 问题: 它旨在解决现有系统在严重组合式真实环境声学失真下产生的"声学鲁棒性瓶颈"，即模型出现内容遗漏与幻觉的问题。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 方法核心包括构建含7种原子现象与54种复合场景的VOICES-IN-THE-WILD-2M数据集、分三阶段的声学到语义渐进监督微调，以及采用WER门控动态融合词元级与句子级奖励的双粒度策略优化。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 实验表明MEGA-ASR在恶劣基准上显著优于最优基线（如VOiCES R4-B-F达45.69%对比54.01%），在复合场景下实现超30%相对WER降低，并通过环境感知路由保持干净语音性能。

Today's Reading Tips 今日阅读推荐

I recommend starting with the RELEX paper, as its analysis of low-rank RLVR dynamics offers a foundational, compute-saving perspective on policy optimization that contextualizes the gated reinforcement learning stages in both IndusAgent and MEGA-ASR. Together, the three papers form a coherent thread on scalable reinforcement learning: RELEX efficiently extrapolates training trajectories, while the other two demonstrate domain-specific instantiations of gated policy optimization in industrial vision and robust speech recognition.
建议首先阅读 RELEX，因为它对低秩 RLVR 动态的分析为高效策略优化提供了基础视角，有助于理解 IndusAgent 和 MEGA-ASR 中的门控强化学习阶段。三篇论文共同构成了可扩展强化学习的连贯脉络：RELEX 实现了训练轨迹的高效外推，而另两篇则分别在工业视觉和鲁棒语音识别中展示了门控策略优化的领域应用。
