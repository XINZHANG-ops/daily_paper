Published: 2026-02-10

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.10388

1. üìò Topic and Domain: This paper focuses on data synthesis for large language model (LLM) post-training, specifically developing methods to measure and improve diversity in training datasets using interpretable feature spaces.
2. üí° Previous Research and New Ideas: The paper builds on existing diversity metrics (text-based measures like distinct-n, embedding distances) and sparse autoencoder (SAE) research, proposing Feature Activation Coverage (FAC) as a new diversity metric that measures coverage in interpretable feature space rather than text space.
3. ‚ùì Problem: The paper aims to solve the challenge of constructing diverse post-training datasets efficiently, addressing limitations of existing diversity metrics that fail to capture task-relevant features driving downstream performance.
4. üõ†Ô∏è Methods: The authors use sparse autoencoders to extract interpretable features, introduce FAC to measure feature coverage, and develop FAC Synthesis - a two-step framework that identifies missing task-relevant features and generates targeted synthetic samples to activate them.
5. üìä Results and Evaluation: The method achieves comparable performance to MAGPIE using 150√ó less data (2K vs 300K samples), shows strong correlation between FAC and downstream performance (Pearson r=0.95), and consistently outperforms baselines across four tasks (toxicity detection, reward modeling, behavior steering, instruction following).
