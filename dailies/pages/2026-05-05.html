Paper 1

MolmoAct2: Action Reasoning Models for Real-world Deployment

Published: 2026-05-03

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.02881

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper presents MolmoAct2, a fully open-source vision-language-action model family designed for real-world robotic manipulation across low-to-medium cost embodiments. 2. **💡 Previous Research and New Ideas:** Building on MolmoAct and vision-language model research, it introduces a spatially specialized VLM backbone (Molmo2-ER), three new large-scale datasets, an open action tokenizer, a flow-matching action expert conditioned on per-layer KV caches, and an adaptive depth reasoning variant (MolmoAct2-Think). 3. **❓ Problem:** It addresses the limitations of existing VLAs—including closed-source weights and data, prohibitive inference latency from explicit reasoning, expensive hardware lock-in, and insufficient fine-tuned success rates for dependable real-world deployment. 4. **🛠️ Methods:** The authors train Molmo2-ER via a specialize-then-rehearse recipe on 3.3M embodied reasoning samples, curate three quality-filtered robot datasets, train an open FAST tokenizer, graft a continuous flow-matching action expert onto a discrete VLM using per-layer KV-cache projections, and implement adaptive depth token prediction that updates only changed scene regions. 5. **📊 Results and Evaluation:** Extensive experiments across seven simulation and real-world benchmarks demonstrate that MolmoAct2 outperforms baselines such as π0.5 in zero-shot deployment, efficient fine-tuning, and trajectory quality, while Molmo2-ER surpasses GPT-5 and Gemini Robotics ER-1.5 on thirteen embodied-reasoning benchmarks.

Large Language Models Explore by Latent Distilling

Published: 2026-04-26

Link: http://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.24927

1. 📘 Topic and Domain: The paper proposes a test-time decoding approach for large language models that encourages semantic exploration, situated in the natural language processing and machine learning domain.
2. 💡 Previous Research and New Ideas: Building on random network distillation and KL-regularized policy optimization, the paper introduces Exploratory Sampling (ESamp), a novel method that uses online latent distillation to detect semantic novelty in continuous hidden representations rather than surface tokens.
3. ❓ Problem: It solves the problem that standard stochastic sampling produces mostly surface-level lexical diversity while underlying reasoning patterns remain semantically redundant, which diminishes the returns of test-time scaling mechanisms like voting or reranking.
4. 🛠️ Methods: The method trains a lightweight Latent Distiller online at test time to predict deep-layer hidden states from shallow-layer ones, uses the prediction error as an intrinsic novelty signal to reweight candidate tokens via KL-regularized optimization, and implements this through an asynchronous training-inference pipeline to minimize overhead.
5. 📊 Results and Evaluation: Empirical results demonstrate that ESamp significantly boosts Pass@k efficiency across mathematical, scientific, and code reasoning benchmarks, breaks the trade-off between diversity and coherence in creative writing, and incurs less than 5% throughput overhead.
1. 📘 主题与领域: 论文提出了一种在大型语言模型测试时生成阶段鼓励语义探索的解码方法，属于自然语言处理与机器学习领域。
2. 💡 先前研究与新思路: 基于随机网络蒸馏和KL正则化策略优化等研究，论文提出了探索性采样（ESamp），其核心创新是通过在线潜在蒸馏在连续隐藏表示空间中检测语义新颖性，而非在表面词元层面操作。
3. ❓ 问题: 论文解决了标准随机采样仅产生表面词汇变化、导致底层推理模式在语义上高度冗余的问题，这种冗余限制了测试时缩放（如投票或重排序）的有效性。
4. 🛠️ 方法: 方法在测试时在线训练一个轻量级潜在蒸馏器来预测从浅层到深层隐藏状态的映射，利用其预测误差作为内在新颖性信号，通过KL正则化优化目标对候选词元进行重加权，并通过异步训练-推理流水线实现以最小化开销。
5. 📊 结果与评估: 实验结果表明，ESamp在数学、科学和代码推理基准上显著提升了Pass@k效率，在创意写作中打破了多样性与连贯性之间的权衡，且吞吐量开销低于5%。
